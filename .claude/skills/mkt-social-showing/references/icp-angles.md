# ICP Angles — make Mark and Mary care

Every package must answer **"why does the ICP care?"** before it ships. Read `brand_context/icp.md`
for the full profile; this maps content to the personas.

## The personas (short)
- **Mark, 47** — runs a £1.8M B2B services firm, 12 staff, built over 18 years. Works 55-80h/wk, *is*
  the business, the bottleneck. Sees Roy living the life (travel, adventure, freedom, fitness) and
  thinks *"I want that — how?"* Tried AI, got generic output, can't see how it fits his business.
- **Mary, 44** — consulting/training firm, £1.2M, team of 8. High standards make delegation feel
  impossible. Open to wellness/growth but has no time.

## Their core pains (hook fuel)
- "I built this thing and now it runs me." (the cage)
- "I'm the bottleneck and I don't know how to stop being it."
- "More revenue just means more hours — I can't scale *me*."
- Health, relationships, identity all quietly paying the price.

## Their aspiration (the pull)
- The bigger life they can see but haven't accessed — freedom, adventure, presence, fitness —
  **without the business falling apart.**

## Mapping content → angle (worked)

| Content | ICP angle (why they care) | Hook direction |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Racing with Spencer** | This is the life you're grinding for but not living. Roy *living it* = proof it's possible. | Identity / aspiration — "the life you're working for" |
| **Scam-warning** | You're switched-on and busy; sophisticated scams target exactly people like you. Roy = trustworthy guide. | Self-relevance + caution |
| **AI / systems content** | "AI never worked for *my* business" — show it applied to a real owner-manager problem. | Contrarian / usefulness |

## Rules
- Pick **one** persona and **one** pain-or-aspiration per post. Trying to speak to everyone speaks to no one.
- The angle is the bridge from *Roy's content* to *their life*. If you can't draw that line, the post is
  a nice video with no job to do — flag it as **Needs Direction**, don't ship.
- Lifestyle/aspiration content (racing, travel, fitness) is on-strategy: it's the *proof* of the promise.
  Tie it back to the work in the caption, never in the video (keeps it from feeling salesy).
