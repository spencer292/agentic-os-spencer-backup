# Viral Framework — the researched methodology

Synthesised from 2025/26 short-form research (sources at the bottom). This is the *why* behind the
skill. Read it once; it explains every move in `SKILL.md`.

## 1. The 3-second law

The opening 1-3 seconds decide whether a video is watched or scrolled past. Benchmarks:
- The best short-form holds **70-90% of viewers through second one**.
- Clearing **60% retention past the 3-second mark** is the threshold that gets a video pushed to
  For You / Reels / the Shorts shelf.
- A hook should land in **3 seconds, ~10-14 words**.

Everything else is downstream of this. A brilliant edit with a slow open dies.

## 2. The hook is three layers, one message

A hook isn't a line — it's **visual + on-screen text + verbal**, all saying the same thing so the
viewer "gets it" instantly. When the three reinforce each other, comprehension is immediate. When
they fight (text says one thing, footage shows another), the viewer bounces.

- **On-screen text** — the literal scroll-stopper, frame 0, big, 4-8 words.
- **Spoken / first caption line** — the verbal hook, 10-14 words.
- **Visual** — the opening shot earns the stop.

## 3. The four triggers (stack at least two)

Every hook works by firing one or more of these. The strongest stack 2+ in one sentence:
1. **Curiosity** — an open loop the brain needs closed ("The one thing nobody tells you about…").
2. **Pattern interrupt** — breaks the expected ("Stop doing X.").
3. **Self-relevance** — "this is about ME / my situation" (name the person or the pain).
4. **Emotional arousal** — surprise, awe, tension, joy, outrage.

## 4. Hook → Retain → Reward (Hormozi)

A content unit has three jobs:
- **Hook** — a bold statement, surprising stat, or pain-point question that jolts attention.
- **Retain** — *one* takeaway. No rambling, no filler, no slow build. Story and tight sections hold
  attention; replays and completion are major ranking signals.
- **Reward** — the viewer must get *more than they expected*: an insight, a feeling, a line they
  repeat. People only feel rewarded if they (1) had a reason to watch, (2) paid attention long
  enough, (3) got that reason satisfied. Deliver on the hook's promise, then a little extra.

## 5. What the algorithms actually reward (2025/26)

- **Watch time + completion rate** is the single heaviest signal across TikTok/Reels/Shorts.
- **Saves and shares are weighted far above likes** — they signal usefulness and "worth passing on."
  Design for a save ("I'll need this") or a share ("this is so them").
- **Search matters** — keyword-rich on-screen text and captions surface content beyond the feed.
- **Longer is fine if completion holds** — 1-3 min can outperform if retention stays high; never pad.

## 6. Shareability triggers (pick one, make it obvious)

People share content that does one of these *for them*:
- **Identity** — "this is me / this is us" (the racing film: the life they want).
- **Relatable truth** — names something they feel but haven't articulated.
- **Usefulness** — they'll come back to it (→ a save).
- **Emotion** — makes them feel something strongly enough to pass it on.

## 7. The non-negotiables

- Hook first, fix it first. A 4/5 edit with a 2/5 hook is a 2/5 post.
- One message per post. Confusion = scroll.
- Earn the stop in the first frame, the hold by 3s, the share by the end.
