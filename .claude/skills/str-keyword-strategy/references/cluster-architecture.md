# Cluster Architecture

> **CLIENT CONTEXT (added 2026-07-02):** worked examples below are from the ATP era (Roy Castleman / THRIVE / Bedford). Apply the METHOD; substitute Got Moles clusters (mole removal, TMCP, cost, DIY-vs-pro, pet safety, city pages) and WA-local geography.

Topic clusters are the architectural unit of 2026 keyword strategy. A cluster = one **pillar page** + a constellation of **supporting queries/pages** that share primary intent and reinforce the pillar's authority.

## What a good cluster looks like

- **3-7 clusters total** for a single-brand foundation. More than 7 = the brand is trying to be all things to all people; fewer than 3 = positioning is too narrow.
- **One pillar page per cluster.** Pillar = the comprehensive answer/hub for the cluster's topic. Existing or to-build, but exactly one.
- **5-15 supporting queries per cluster.** These are the queries the pillar's child/cornerstone pages target.
- **Shared primary intent across the cluster.** A cluster mixing Informational + Transactional dilutes the topical authority signal.
- **Cluster boundaries match user mental models, not content production convenience.** If users would search across two clusters in one session, you may have drawn the boundaries wrong.

## How clusters derive from positioning

Positioning pillars → clusters. Roy's positioning has three pillars:
- AI thinking → cluster `ai-thinking`
- Wellness for owners → cluster `wellness-for-owners`
- BOS UP business operating system → cluster `bos-up`

Plus brand/credential clusters that don't map to a single positioning pillar but matter for citation:
- `roy-castleman-author-credential` (book + author entity queries)
- `bedford-uk-local` (regional subset, supplementary)

Five clusters total. Sits comfortably in the 3-7 range.

## Pillar vs supporting page anatomy

| Layer | Job | Length | Schema |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pillar | Comprehensive overview of the cluster topic. Links out to all supporting pages. | 2,500-5,000 words | Article + WebSite + speakable |
| Supporting (cornerstone) | Deep dive on one sub-topic of the pillar. Links back to pillar. | 1,500-3,000 words | Article + relevant specific schema (HowTo for instruction intent, FAQPage for short fact, etc.) |
| Snippet/answer page | Single specific question, often an FAQ or definition. | 300-800 words | Article + FAQPage |

**Rule:** every supporting page must link to its pillar. Every pillar must link to all supporting pages. This is what creates the topical authority signal.

## Cluster construction process

1. **Start with positioning pillars** — each pillar likely seeds one cluster
2. **Add brand/credential clusters** — book launches, podcast hubs, founder-as-entity get their own cluster
3. **Add regional subsets if applicable** — Bedford-local, UK-regional, etc., as separate clusters not folded into main clusters
4. **Stress-test with ICP queries** — would the ICP search across clusters in one session? If yes, boundaries may need adjusting
5. **Check cluster size** — if a cluster has fewer than 5 supporting queries, consider whether it's really a cluster or just a satellite of another

## Anti-patterns

- **Multi-pillar clusters** — "thinking with AI AND wellness AND BOS UP" — too broad. Each gets its own cluster.
- **Single-page clusters** — one URL with no supporting pages = not a cluster, just a page. Either build supporting content or absorb into the nearest cluster.
- **Audience-only clusters** — "for service business owners" isn't a cluster (that's the ICP, not a topic). Cluster by topic, then ensure every cluster speaks to the ICP.
- **Format-only clusters** — "blog posts" isn't a cluster. Format is downstream of topic.
- **Funnel-stage clusters** — "TOFU / MOFU / BOFU" isn't a cluster either. Intent within a topic determines pages, not the other way around.
