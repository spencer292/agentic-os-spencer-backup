# GEO Optimization Checklist

How to structure content so AI search engines (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) can parse, extract, and cite it.

---

## Why This Matters

AI search engines don't just rank pages — they extract answers and cite sources. Content structured for GEO gets cited directly in AI-generated responses, which drives traffic even in zero-click environments.

Got Moles has **zero competitors doing GEO** in the mole control space. Every well-structured piece of content has a first-mover advantage.

---

## Structure Checklist

Run through this for every post before saving:

### 1. BLUF Paragraph
- [ ] First paragraph directly answers the primary search query
- [ ] Contains at least one specific number or fact
- [ ] 2-3 sentences max — AI extracts this as the "quick answer"
- [ ] Could stand alone as a complete answer if nothing else was read

### 2. Heading Hierarchy
- [ ] H1 = title (includes primary keyword)
- [ ] H2s match actual search queries people type ("How deep do moles dig?" not "Depth Considerations")
- [ ] H3s break complex sections into scannable sub-topics
- [ ] No heading skips (H1 → H3 without H2)
- [ ] Each H2 section is independently useful — a reader jumping to it via search gets value

### 3. Comparison Tables
- [ ] Include at least one table for any post comparing options, species, products, or methods
- [ ] Tables have clear headers and consistent formatting
- [ ] Data is specific (numbers, names, facts) not vague ("varies", "depends")
- [ ] AI parses tables extremely well — this is one of the highest-value GEO structures

### 4. Numbered Lists
- [ ] Steps, processes, or rankings use numbered lists (not bullets)
- [ ] Each item starts with the key point (not a filler word)
- [ ] AI extracts numbered lists as featured snippets and step-by-step answers

### 5. Definitions and Key Facts
- [ ] Important terms are defined inline or in a clear definition format
- [ ] Key facts are stated as standalone sentences (not buried in paragraphs)
- [ ] Format: "[Term]: [definition]" or "**[Term]** — [definition]"
- [ ] AI loves extracting clean definitions

### 6. FAQ Section
- [ ] 3-5 FAQs at the end of every post
- [ ] Questions match actual search queries from the search intent map
- [ ] Answers are 2-4 sentences — specific enough to be useful, short enough to be quoted
- [ ] Each Q&A pair is schema-ready (can be wrapped in FAQ structured data)
- [ ] AI search engines extract FAQ pairs directly — this is the #1 GEO structure

### 7. Citation-Worthy Statements
- [ ] Include at least 2-3 statements that are definitive enough to be cited
- [ ] Format: factual claim + specific evidence. "Moles eat 60-80% of their body weight daily in earthworms" not "Moles eat a lot of worms"
- [ ] Attribute claims to sources where possible (WSU Extension, WDFW, company data)
- [ ] AI trusts and cites content that names its sources

### 8. Local Signals (for local content)
- [ ] City/county/region names appear naturally in content
- [ ] Local conditions mentioned (soil types, climate, species)
- [ ] Service area referenced with specific coverage details
- [ ] AI search gives local content priority for local queries

## What AI Search Engines Extract

Understanding what gets extracted helps structure content for citation:

| Structure | How AI Uses It | Priority |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| BLUF paragraph | Quick answer / featured snippet | Highest |
| FAQ Q&A pairs | Direct extraction for question-answer queries | Highest |
| Comparison tables | Side-by-side answers for "vs" queries | High |
| Numbered lists | Step-by-step answers for "how to" queries | High |
| Definitions | Direct definitions for "what is" queries | High |
| Statistics with sources | Cited facts in AI responses | Medium |
| H2 headings that match queries | Section-level extraction | Medium |

---

## Anti-Patterns (Things That Hurt GEO)

- **Vague language:** "Many experts agree" — name the expert or cite the source
- **Buried answers:** Making the reader scroll past 500 words of context before getting the answer
- **Opinion without evidence:** "We believe..." — state facts, let the reader draw conclusions
- **Keyword stuffing:** Repeating the primary keyword unnaturally — AI detects this and downgrades
- **Thin content:** Posts under 800 words rarely get cited — not enough depth to be the definitive source
- **No unique data:** If everything in the post is available on 10 other sites, AI has no reason to cite this one. Proprietary knowledge base facts are the differentiator.

---

## GEO vs Traditional SEO

GEO doesn't replace SEO — it layers on top. Everything that works for traditional SEO still matters:

| Traditional SEO | GEO Addition |
|----------------|-------------|
| Title tag with keyword | BLUF paragraph that answers the query |
| Meta description | FAQ section with schema-ready Q&A |
| Header hierarchy | Headers that match actual search queries |
| Internal links | Comparison tables and numbered lists |
| Alt text on images | Citation-worthy statistics with sources |
| Mobile-friendly | Structured definitions and key facts |

Do both. Always.
