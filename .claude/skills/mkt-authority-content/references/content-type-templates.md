# Content Type Templates

Each template below defines the structure for a specific content type. Match the template to the content type selected in Step 1.

---

## Guide Template

The comprehensive overview. Longest format. For broad topics where the reader needs the full picture.

**When to use:** "Complete guide to...", "Everything you need to know about...", "What is [topic]?"

```
# [Title — include primary keyword]

[BLUF: 2-3 sentences answering the core question. Include one specific number or fact.]

## [Context Section — why this matters]
[1-2 paragraphs setting up why the reader should care. Reference their situation.]

## [Deep Section 1 — H2 matches a search query]
[Detailed coverage. Use knowledge base facts. Include comparison table if relevant.]

## [Deep Section 2]
[Continue depth. Each H2 should be independently useful — someone jumping to this section via search should get value.]

## [Deep Section 3]
[Additional depth as needed. 3-6 H2 sections typical for guides.]

## [What This Means for You / What to Do Next]
[Practical takeaway. Brand mentioned naturally as the authority, not a pitch.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Question matching real search query]?
[2-4 sentence answer with a specific fact.]

### [Question 2]?
[Answer.]

### [Question 3]?
[Answer.]
```

**Word count:** 2000-2500
**Tone:** Authoritative but accessible. "Here's what we know" energy.

## Myth-Bust Template

Direct, punchy. For topics where the common belief is wrong and the reader is about to waste money.

**When to use:** "Does [X] work for...", "Is it true that...", "Debunking [myth]"

```
# [Title — state the myth or question directly]

[BLUF: 2 sentences. State the answer immediately. "No, [X] does not work. Here's why."]

## The Myth
[1 paragraph: what people believe and where they heard it. Be empathetic — this belief is reasonable.]

## Why People Believe It
[1-2 paragraphs: the logic seems sound on the surface. Acknowledge the reasoning.]

## The Real Answer
[2-3 paragraphs: the facts, with evidence. Cite sources (WSU Extension, specific data). Include a comparison table if comparing methods.]

## What Actually Works
[1-2 paragraphs: the effective alternative. Brand mentioned naturally as practitioners of the effective method.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Related myth question]?
[Direct answer.]

### [Practical follow-up question]?
[Answer.]

### [Cost/effort question]?
[Answer.]
```

**Word count:** 1200-1800
**Tone:** Straight-talking. Not condescending about the myth — empathetic about why it's believed, definitive about the truth.

## Comparison Template

Side-by-side analysis. For topics where the reader is choosing between options.

**When to use:** "[X] vs [Y]", "Which is better...", "DIY vs professional..."

```
# [Title — include both options]

[BLUF: 2-3 sentences. State which option is better for which situation.]

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | [Option A] | [Option B] |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| [Factor 1] | [Detail] | [Detail] |
| [Factor 2] | [Detail] | [Detail] |
| [Factor 3] | [Detail] | [Detail] |
| [Factor 4] | [Detail] | [Detail] |
| **Best for** | [Summary] | [Summary] |

## [Option A]: The Details
[2-3 paragraphs with honest pros and cons.]

## [Option B]: The Details
[2-3 paragraphs with honest pros and cons.]

## When [Option A] Makes Sense
[Specific scenarios. Be honest — if the cheaper/DIY option works sometimes, say so.]

## When [Option B] Makes Sense
[Specific scenarios. Brand positioned naturally if relevant.]

## The Verdict
[1-2 paragraphs. Honest recommendation based on the reader's likely situation.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Decision question]?
[Answer.]

### [Cost question]?
[Answer.]

### [Follow-up question]?
[Answer.]
```

**Word count:** 1500-2000
**Tone:** Fair and balanced. The reader trusts the comparison because it's honest about both sides.

## How-To Template

Step-by-step instructions. For practical topics where the reader wants to do something.

**When to use:** "How to...", "Step-by-step guide to...", "What to do when..."

```
# [Title — "How to [Action]" format]

[BLUF: 2 sentences. What the reader will accomplish and the key thing to know before starting.]

## What You'll Need
[Bullet list of tools, materials, or knowledge required.]

## Step 1: [Action]
[1-2 paragraphs. Clear instruction with specific details.]

## Step 2: [Action]
[Repeat for each step. Include tips or warnings where relevant.]

## Step 3: [Action]
[Continue. 4-7 steps typical.]

## Tips for Better Results
[Bullet list of pro tips from the knowledge base.]

## When to Call a Professional
[Honest guidance on when DIY isn't enough. Brand mentioned naturally.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [How long does this take]?
[Answer.]

### [Common mistake question]?
[Answer.]

### [Cost question]?
[Answer.]
```

**Word count:** 1200-2000
**Tone:** Practical and helpful. Like a knowledgeable friend explaining how to do something.

## Seasonal Template

Time-specific advice. For content tied to seasons, months, or weather patterns.

**When to use:** "When are [X] most active...", "[Season] guide to...", "Is [month] a good time for..."

```
# [Title — include season/time and location if relevant]

[BLUF: 2-3 sentences. What's happening this season and what the reader should know.]

## What's Happening Right Now
[1-2 paragraphs on the biology/behavior driving seasonal changes. Knowledge base facts.]

## [Season 1]: [What to expect]
[Detailed breakdown of this period. Include months.]

## [Season 2]: [What to expect]
[Continue through relevant seasons.]

## What This Means for Your [Property/Lawn/Garden]
[Practical implications. What should the reader do or watch for?]

## The Best Time to Act
[Direct recommendation. When to call a professional vs. wait.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Timing question]?
[Answer.]

### [Season-specific question]?
[Answer.]

### [Action question]?
[Answer.]
```

**Word count:** 1500-2000
**Tone:** Timely and specific. The reader should feel like this content is written for right now.

## Local Template

Area-specific content. For city pages, regional guides, or location-targeted posts.

**When to use:** "[Service] in [City]", "[Topic] in [Region]", "Why [City] has a [problem]"

```
# [Title — include city/region name and topic]

[BLUF: 2-3 sentences. Answer the local question with area-specific facts.]

## Why [City/Region] Is [Affected/Unique]
[1-2 paragraphs on local conditions: soil types, climate, proximity to nature, typical lot sizes.]

## What [City] Residents Should Know
[2-3 specific facts relevant to this area. Use knowledge base for species/soil/seasonal data.]

## [Service/Solution] Options in [City]
[What's available locally. Brand mentioned if serving this area.]

## Local Tips
[Area-specific advice that generic content can't provide.]

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does [service] cost in [city]?
[Local pricing if available, otherwise regional range.]

### Are [problem] common in [city]?
[Yes/no with local context.]

### What kind of [species/issue] does [city] have?
[Specific local answer.]
```

**Word count:** 1000-1500
**Tone:** Local and specific. The reader should feel like this was written by someone who knows their area.
