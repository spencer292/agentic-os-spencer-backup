## Major Studies

### Zyppy — 23 Million Internal Links (Cyrus Shepard, 2024)

The largest internal linking study to date. 23 million internal links across 1,800 websites (~520,000 URLs), cross-referenced with Google Search Console data.

| Finding | Data |
|---------|------|
| Optimal internal links per page | 40-50 for maximum organic clicks |
| Links beyond 50 | Reversed the benefit |
| Exact-match anchor impact | Pages with at least 1 exact-match anchor = **5x more traffic** |
| Anchor text variety | Extremely strong correlation with clicks (confirmed 3x) |
| URL/naked anchors | ~50% more traffic than pages without |
| Anchor variation ceiling | Data unreliable after 25 variations per URL |

## Anchor Text Benchmarks

**Recommended distribution:**
- 40% partial match (keyword within natural phrase)
- 40% descriptive (describes destination naturally)
- 20% exact match (uses exact target keyword)

**Optimal anchor length:** 4.85 words / 23.5 characters average. Under 100 characters correlates with higher rankings. Google recommends 2-5 words.

**Over-optimization threshold:** >80% exact match to a single page triggers spam signals.

## Google's Position (Current)

**John Mueller:**
- Internal linking is "super-critical for SEO"
- "One of the biggest things you can do on a website"
- "The number of clicks it takes to get to a page is more important than your URL structure"
- Recommends pyramid structure

**December 2025 Helpful Content Update:**
- Sites with strategic internal linking performed significantly better
- Google using internal linking as a strong signal for topic authority
- Clear content hierarchies rewarded: "This is our definitive guide on X. These five articles provide supporting detail."

**Technical requirements:**
- Links must use `<a>` elements with `href` attributes
- Search engines don't use search boxes or pulldown menus
- Every page you care about needs at least one link from another page
- Nofollow on internal links: equity dissipates (doesn't redistribute)

## AI Search Engine Citation Impact (2026)

Internal linking has become a primary signal for AI answer engines (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Bing Copilot) to identify which pages within a topical cluster are authoritative enough to cite.

| Finding | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| AI citation rate uplift from hub-and-spoke topical clustering | 12% baseline → 41% with proper hub-spoke | TopicalMap.ai 2026 |
| Pillar page should link bidirectionally with cluster pages | 8-12 cluster pages per pillar | Industry consensus 2026 |
| AI engines weight contextual body links 3-5x higher than nav/footer | Confirmed across multiple LLM analyses | 12AM Agency / Conductor 2026 |
| Local-intent AI Overview presence | 76.9% of "near me" informational queries trigger AIO | Whitespark / Conductor 2026 |
| Pure transactional Map Pack queries triggering AIO | Only 7% | Whitespark 2026 |

**Implications:**
- For local-service businesses, AI citation surface is informational, not transactional
- Hub-spoke architecture matters more for AI citation than for traditional SEO
- Internal links from blog content TO city + service pages are the highest-leverage AI-citation signal a local business can build
- Anchor diversity on pillar→cluster links correlates with AI citation breadth

## Anchor Text Distribution — 2026 Update

The 2025 standard "40% partial / 40% descriptive / 20% exact" framing is being superseded by a 4-category model that explicitly accounts for branded anchors:

| Anchor Type | Recommended Share | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Branded | 40% | "Got Moles", "the Got Moles team", brand+context |
| Keyword (exact + partial) | 30% | "mole control Seattle", "professional mole trapping" |
| Generic / LSI | 30% | "see our process", "compare options", "learn more" |

**Sources:** 12AM Agency (2026), Carnegie Higher Ed (2026), LinkDoctor (2026), iBeam Consulting (2026).

Within the 30% keyword bucket, target 15-25% as exact-match to a single priority page (Zyppy 5x traffic finding).

**Sitewide rule:** flag any single anchor text used >5 times across the site — repetition without diversity reads as anchor-spam to sophisticated crawlers and dilutes signal value (per Roy's strategy doc §6 + `feedback_per_post_topical_linking.md`).
