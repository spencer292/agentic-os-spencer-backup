# Earned Media + Linkable Assets

Steps 6 and 7 of `str-authority-strategy`. Together: digital PR is the #1 link-building method in 2026 (48.6% of SEO professionals); linkable assets are the supply side that makes earned media work.

## Why these two pair together

Earned media and linkable assets are not separate workstreams. They're two sides of the same machine:
- **Linkable asset** = the content people want to cite
- **Earned media** = where they cite it

A linkable asset without distribution is invisible. An earned-media push without a linkable asset is begging — and journalists ignore it. The pair is the engine.

## Earned-media target list (Step 6)

For each cluster from `target-keywords.md`, list 10-15 publications/podcasts/platforms that pass three filters:

1. **Topic coverage** — they regularly cover the cluster's subject
2. **Audience overlap** — the brand's ICP reads/listens
3. **Authority that matters** — DA / brand recognition / AI citation footprint

### Tiering by access difficulty

| Tier | Example outlet types | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — flagship** | Wired, HBR, FT, NYT, BBC, Bloomberg | Original-research + journalist relationships + Tier 2/3 stack first as proof |
| **Tier 2 — industry/trade** | Real Business, Inc., Entrepreneur, Forbes contributor network, industry associations | Achievable with strong original data, expert quote, or contributed byline |
| **Tier 3 — niche / podcast / specialist** | Podcasts, niche industry blogs, expert-quote platforms (Featured, Qwoted) | Most achievable, accumulated volume creates compound brand-mention authority |

Most strategies fail by chasing Tier 1 too early. Build Tier 3 stack first → Tier 2 follows → Tier 1 reachable with proof points.

### Per-outlet capture
- **Outlet name**
- **Tier** (1/2/3)
- **Topic match** (which cluster + why)
- **Audience overlap** (full / partial — which ICP segment)
- **Pitch angle** (the specific story this outlet would publish — NOT generic)
- **Submission/contact path** (editorial guidelines URL, journalist beat coverage, podcast booking page)
- **Status** (target / contacted / pitched / placed)

### Anti-patterns
- **Generic outlet lists.** "Top 50 business publications" without per-outlet pitch angle = unused
- **Country-prefix bias.** Default global; only flag a regional outlet as primary if there's deliberate regional intent
- **Chasing dead outlets.** Some Tier 1 brands no longer publish guest content. Verify editorial guidelines current
- **Counting reach without authority.** A 100K-reader outlet with 0 AI citation footprint matters less than a 20K-reader outlet that AI cites

## Linkable asset patterns (Step 7)

Linkable assets are content built specifically because they're cite-worthy. The hook (why journalists/AI cite it) matters more than the format.

### Working asset types

| Type | Why it earns citations | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Original survey** (n=200+) | Quotable stat, "according to {brand} research" | "{Brand}'s 2026 Service Business Owner AI Adoption Index" |
| **Industry data study** | Aggregates public + private data into a definitive number | "What service businesses spend on AI tools per quarter — analysis of 1,200 owners" |
| **Definitive guide** | Comprehensive enough to become the canonical reference | "The Complete Guide to {Cluster Topic}" — 8,000 words, deeply linked |
| **Calculator / interactive tool** | Functional utility people share + cite | "Service Business Owner Burnout Score" |
| **Named framework with HowTo schema** | Becomes citeable methodology | T.H.R.I.V.E. method, EVOLVE method, CARE framework |
| **Comparison framework** | Side-by-side that becomes the standard reference | "{Topic} comparison: 8 frameworks evaluated against 12 criteria" |
| **Original taxonomy** | New way to categorise the topic | Roy's Double Burnout naming |

### Hook engineering

Every linkable asset needs a hook — the angle that makes it more citeable than a generic guide. Hooks come in five shapes:

1. **Original number** — "X% of [audience] do Y" backed by primary research
2. **Counter-narrative** — "Most coaches teach X. Here's why it doesn't work for service businesses"
3. **First-of-kind** — first comprehensive treatment, first measurement, first comparison
4. **Insider data** — proprietary access (your own client base, your own platform data)
5. **Synthesis** — pulling 5+ existing research threads into one definitive picture

A guide without a hook is content. A guide with a hook is a linkable asset.

### Effort sizing
- **Trivial** — repurpose existing data into a structured asset (definitive guide from existing pillar pages, framework write-up)
- **Moderate** — original survey with n=100-300, industry data scrape + analysis, calculator
- **Substantial** — original research with n=500+, multi-month data study, peer-reviewed methodology

Match effort to the cluster's authority gap. Don't build substantial assets for clusters that already have strong topical coverage.

### Per-asset capture
- **Asset type + title**
- **Cluster served** (must align with `target-keywords.md` cluster id)
- **Hook** (one sentence — why this earns citations)
- **Effort** (trivial / moderate / substantial)
- **Distribution plan** (which Tier 1/2/3 outlets get the pitch when it's ready)
- **Reusability** (can it be sliced into LinkedIn posts, podcast episodes, carousels — see `mkt-content-repurposing`)
- **Status** (planned / in production / published / amplifying)

## How earned media and linkable assets execute together

Sequence per cluster:
1. Pick the cluster's primary linkable asset (one substantial, one moderate, one trivial — phased)
2. Build the trivial asset first → use as proof point for outlet conversations
3. Build moderate asset next → distribute to Tier 3 + Tier 2
4. Build substantial asset last → distribute to Tier 1 with Tier 2/3 stack as proof
5. Repurpose every asset into LinkedIn posts, podcast topics, carousel slides per `mkt-content-repurposing`
6. Track every placement in the press log

Every linkable asset is the spine of a 6-12 month earned-media campaign per cluster. Without the asset, the earned-media list is a wish list.

## What `mkt-content-repurposing` does NOT replace

The repurposing skill atomises content for social/platform reach. It does NOT generate the original linkable asset. The linkable asset is the foundation; the repurposed atoms amplify it. Both are needed; neither replaces the other.
