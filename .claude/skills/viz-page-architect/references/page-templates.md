# Page Templates — Validated Section Ordering

Each page type has a validated section order based on how visitors make decisions.
Sections are numbered. Each section includes its job, content requirements, CTA guidance,
social proof guidance, the conversion principle it serves (LIFT model), and mobile notes.

LIFT model factors: Relevance, Clarity, Urgency, Anxiety reduction, Distraction removal.

---

## Homepage

The front door. Answers five questions in five seconds: Who is this for? What outcome
do I get? Why is this different? What proof exists? What do I do next?

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Answer the 5 questions. Earn the scroll.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: outcome-focused, specific to ICP. Not clever. Clear.
- Subheadline: expand on the headline. Who this is for and what changes.
- Primary CTA button: action + benefit (e.g., "Start your free assessment")
- Supporting visual: professional photo, product shot, or subtle motion. Not stock.
- Estimated words: 30-50

**CTA:** Primary. One button. No competing links in the hero.
**Social proof:** Optional micro-proof. One metric or "Trusted by N+ [type]" line.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Clarity. Visitor decides in 5 seconds if this is for them.
**Mobile:** Headline and CTA must be visible without scrolling. Image crops to portrait or hides. CTA in thumb zone.

### 2. Social Proof Bar

**Job:** Borrow credibility before the visitor invests attention.

**Content requirements:**
- 4-6 logos in grayscale (publications, clients, certifications)
- OR 2-3 key metrics (years in business, clients served, results achieved)
- No explanatory text needed. Logos speak for themselves.
- Estimated words: 0-15

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** This IS the social proof. Logo bar or metrics only.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "Other credible entities trust this."
**Mobile:** Horizontal scroll or 2-row grid. Logos scale down. Metrics stack vertically.

### 3. Problem / Pain Articulation

**Job:** Show the visitor you understand their world better than they can describe it.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: name the frustration directly
- 3-5 pain points in their language (pull from ICP "how they describe it")
- Concrete details. Numbers. Specific scenarios. Not vague empathy.
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None. This section earns trust, not clicks.
**Social proof:** None. Pure empathy. Proof comes after the solution.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance. "This person understands my exact situation."
**Mobile:** Full width. No columns. Pain points as stacked blocks or cards.

### 4. Solution Overview

**Job:** Show how you solve it. Outcomes, not features.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: transformation statement (from X to Y)
- 3 outcome pillars or key benefits. Each with icon, short title, 1-2 sentence explanation.
- Focus on what changes for them, not what you do.
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** Secondary. Soft text link ("See how it works" or "Explore the method").
**Social proof:** None. Save proof for the next section.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity. "Now I understand what this actually does."
**Mobile:** Pillars stack vertically. Icons above text. Full-width cards.

### 5. Deep Proof

**Job:** Remove doubt with specific evidence.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 testimonials with specific results (numbers, timeframes, named outcomes)
- Format: quote + name + role + result summary
- OR 1 case study card with before/after metrics
- Testimonials should address different objections (time, money, scepticism)
- Estimated words: 150-300

**CTA:** None. Let the proof land.
**Social proof:** This IS the proof section. Testimonials with specifics.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "People like me got results."
**Mobile:** Testimonials as swipeable cards or stacked full-width.

### 6. How It Works

**Job:** Demystify the process. Make it feel doable.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: "How it works" or similar
- 3-step process (maximum 4). Each step: number, title, 1-sentence explanation.
- The steps should feel simple even if the actual process is complex.
- Optional: timeline or duration indicator
- Estimated words: 60-120

**CTA:** Primary. After demystifying, the visitor is ready to act. "Get started" or equivalent.
**Social proof:** None. Simplicity IS the persuasion here.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction. "This isn't complicated. I can do this."
**Mobile:** Steps stack vertically with numbers prominent. CTA full-width below.

### 7. About / Credibility

**Job:** Give reason to trust the person behind the offer.

**Content requirements:**
- Professional photo (not stock)
- 2-3 sentences: who you are, why you built this, what qualifies you
- Credential highlights (years, certifications, publications). Not a full biography.
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** Secondary. Text link to full About page.
**Social proof:** Credentials embedded in copy. Not a separate element.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "A real person with real experience is behind this."
**Mobile:** Photo above text. Full width. Credentials as a compact list.

### 8. Secondary CTA

**Job:** Catch visitors who are interested and not ready to commit to the primary action.

**Content requirements:**
- Lower-commitment offer: newsletter, free resource, quiz, email course
- Headline: benefit of the secondary action
- Brief explanation: what they get, how often, no spam promise
- Form: email field + button (2 fields maximum)
- Estimated words: 30-60

**CTA:** Secondary. Different visual treatment from primary CTA. Less prominent.
**Social proof:** Optional. Subscriber count or "Join N+ [people]" if available.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "I can engage without committing."
**Mobile:** Full-width form. Large input fields. Button in thumb zone.

### 9. FAQ

**Job:** Handle the top 3-5 objections that prevent action.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 questions maximum on homepage. Link to full FAQ page if more exist.
- Questions phrased as the visitor would ask them (not marketing speak)
- Answers: direct, concise, objection-resolving. End each with confidence.
- Implement FAQPage schema markup
- Estimated words: 150-300

**CTA:** None in the FAQ itself. The next section handles the final push.
**Social proof:** Embed proof in answers where relevant ("Most clients see results within...")
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "My specific concern was addressed."
**Mobile:** Accordion pattern. One question open at a time. Tap to expand.

### 10. Footer

**Job:** Provide navigation, legal links, and final contact info without clutter.

**Content requirements:**
- Secondary navigation: links to key pages
- Contact info: email, phone (if applicable), location
- Social media links
- Legal: privacy policy, terms, cookie policy
- Copyright line
- Estimated words: 30-50

**CTA:** None. The footer is utility, not persuasion.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Distraction removal. Clean closure, not another sales pitch.
**Mobile:** Single column. Links stacked. Social icons in a row.

## About / Founder Page

The trust-builder. Opens with fire, not a calm beginning. The visitor needs to know
why this person is worth listening to.

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Establish identity in one frame. Professional photo plus one-line identity statement.

**Content requirements:**
- Professional photo (not stock, not overly staged)
- One-line identity statement: who you are and who you serve
- No CTA in the hero. This page earns trust first.
- Estimated words: 10-20

**CTA:** None. Trust comes before action on this page.
**Social proof:** None. The photo and statement are the first impression.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance. "This is a real person who does what I need."
**Mobile:** Photo full-width above text. Statement as overlay or below.

### 2. Origin Story

**Job:** Create emotional connection through the "why" narrative.

**Content requirements:**
- Open with the fire, not the calm beginning. Start at the turning point or the struggle.
- First-person voice. Vulnerable and specific.
- Connect the personal story to the audience's situation. "I know what it's like because..."
- Estimated words: 200-400

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** The story itself IS proof of understanding.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Anxiety reduction. "This person has been where I am."
**Mobile:** Full-width text. No columns. Generous line spacing for readability.

### 3. Turning Point

**Job:** Show the moment everything changed. This is what the audience wants for themselves.

**Content requirements:**
- One pivotal moment or realisation. Specific, not vague.
- What changed as a result. Concrete outcomes.
- Bridge to the method: "This is why I built [approach]."
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None. The narrative carries this section.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity. "I see how this person's experience connects to what they offer."
**Mobile:** Same as origin story. Continuous reading flow.

### 4. Credibility Markers

**Job:** Provide hard evidence of qualification and experience.

**Content requirements:**
- Specific numbers: years of experience, clients served, revenue generated, results achieved
- Certifications, publications, speaking engagements
- Format: grid of metrics or a compact credentials list
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Credentials ARE social proof. Specific numbers only. No vague claims.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "This person is genuinely qualified."
**Mobile:** Metrics in a 2-column grid. Credentials as a bulleted list.

### 5. Philosophy / Approach

**Job:** Differentiate from competitors. Show what makes the approach unique.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 core beliefs or principles that drive the work
- Frame as contrast: "Most [industry] does X. We do Y because [reason]."
- Connect beliefs to outcomes. Philosophy is only interesting if it produces results.
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Optional. One testimonial that validates the philosophy.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Clarity. "This person thinks differently, and that's why they get results."
**Mobile:** Principles as stacked cards with titles and short explanations.

### 6. Personal Touches

**Job:** Build human connection. Show the person behind the professional.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 personal details: hobbies, family, values, quirks
- Photos that show personality (not just headshots)
- Keep it brief. This supports, not replaces, the professional narrative.
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None. This is about being human, not proving credentials.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "I like this person. I'd enjoy working with them."
**Mobile:** Photo gallery as horizontal scroll or grid.

### 7. CTA

**Job:** Convert the trust earned above into action.

**Content requirements:**
- Primary CTA: book a call, take the quiz, start an assessment
- Frame the CTA as the logical next step after reading the story
- Brief friction reducer: "No commitment. Just a conversation."
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Primary. One clear action. This is the only CTA on the page.
**Social proof:** Optional. One-line testimonial near the CTA.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction. "I trust this person. The next step is easy."
**Mobile:** Full-width button in thumb zone. Friction reducer text visible above the fold.

## Service / Programme Page

Sells the transformation, not the deliverables. Leads with who you help and what they get.

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Name who you help and the specific outcome they get. Not what you do, what they get.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: outcome for the ICP. Specific.
- Subheadline: who this is for and what changes
- Primary CTA: "Get started" or "Book a call"
- Estimated words: 30-50

**CTA:** Primary.
**Social proof:** Optional one-line result ("Clients reclaim 15+ hours per week on average").
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Clarity.
**Mobile:** Headline and CTA visible without scrolling.

### 2. Problem Articulation

**Job:** Show you understand their world.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 specific pain points in their language
- Concrete scenarios, not abstract frustrations
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance.
**Mobile:** Pain points as stacked cards.

### 3. Solution / Offer

**Job:** Explain what's included in plain language.

**Content requirements:**
- What's included: modules, sessions, deliverables, access
- Format each item with a benefit, not just a feature name
- Estimated words: 150-300

**CTA:** Secondary. "See full details" or similar.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Inclusions as expandable accordion items.

### 4. How It Works

**Job:** Demystify the process.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-4 step process with timeline
- Each step: what happens, what they do, what they get
- Estimated words: 80-150

**CTA:** Primary after the process. "Start step 1" or equivalent.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Steps stacked vertically with step numbers.

### 5. Proof

**Job:** Show results from real people.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 testimonials addressing different concerns
- Case study snippet with before/after metrics
- Estimated words: 150-300

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Testimonials with specific results and named outcomes.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Swipeable testimonial cards.

### 6. Objection Handling

**Job:** Address the reasons people don't buy.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 common objections as FAQ items
- Direct, confident answers. Not defensive.
- Estimated words: 150-300

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Embed proof in answers where it resolves the objection.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Accordion pattern.

### 7. Pricing / Investment

**Job:** Frame the value, not the cost. (Include only if pricing is shown on this page.)

**Content requirements:**
- Price with value framing: "Less than the cost of [relatable comparison]"
- What's included summary (repeat key items)
- Payment options if available
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** Primary. "Enrol now" or "Book a call to discuss".
**Social proof:** ROI testimonial near pricing.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Price prominent. CTA full-width. Inclusions as compact list.

### 8. Final CTA

**Job:** Close the page with one clear action.

**Content requirements:**
- Repeat the primary CTA with a fresh benefit angle
- Friction reducer: guarantee, no-commitment offer, "questions? email us"
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Primary.
**Social proof:** Optional final testimonial.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction + Urgency (if applicable).
**Mobile:** Full-width CTA in thumb zone.

## Pricing Page

Frame the value before showing the cost. Centre-stage effect: highlight the recommended tier.

### 1. Value Headline

**Job:** Frame the investment in terms of what they get, not what they pay.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: "Choose your path to [outcome]" or similar
- Subheadline: brief value context
- Estimated words: 15-30

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Clarity.
**Mobile:** Centred text. No columns.

### 2. Pricing Tiers

**Job:** Present options with a clear recommendation.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 tiers (3 preferred for centre-stage effect)
- Each tier: name, price, headline benefit, feature list, CTA button
- Recommended tier visually highlighted (larger, different colour, "Most popular" badge)
- Annual/monthly toggle if applicable
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** Primary on recommended tier. Secondary on others.
**Social proof:** "Most popular" or "Best value" badge on recommended tier.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Distraction removal. Easy comparison.
**Mobile:** Tiers stack vertically. Recommended tier first or marked clearly.

### 3. Feature Comparison

**Job:** Help detail-oriented buyers compare. (Optional. Include only if tiers are complex.)

**Content requirements:**
- Comparison table with checkmarks
- Group features by category
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** CTA button at bottom of each column repeating tier selection.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Horizontal scroll table or stacked comparison cards.

### 4. Social Proof Near Pricing

**Job:** Reduce price anxiety with proof of value.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 testimonials specifically about value, ROI, or "worth every penny"
- Testimonials from people similar to ICP
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** ROI-focused testimonials.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Swipeable cards.

### 5. Pricing FAQ

**Job:** Handle pricing-specific objections.

**Content requirements:**
- 5-7 questions about pricing: refund policy, payment plans, what's included,
  how long access lasts, cancellation
- Direct, confident answers
- Estimated words: 200-350

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Embed proof in answers where relevant.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Accordion pattern.

### 6. Bottom CTA

**Job:** Final conversion opportunity after all objections handled.

**Content requirements:**
- Repeat recommended tier CTA
- Friction reducer: guarantee, trial period, "talk to us first"
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Primary.
**Social proof:** Optional. "Join N+ [people who chose this]."
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction + Clarity.
**Mobile:** Full-width CTA. Sticky bottom bar option for long pages.

## Book / Product Landing Page

Single product showcase. Hero sells, body proves, CTA repeats.

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Show the product and make the primary action obvious.

**Content requirements:**
- Product image (book cover, product shot) — high quality, styled
- Headline: the promise or transformation the product delivers
- Primary CTA: buy, pre-order, download
- Price if applicable
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Primary. Buy/pre-order/download.
**Social proof:** Optional. "N copies sold" or endorsement quote.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Relevance.
**Mobile:** Product image above CTA. Both visible without scrolling.

### 2. Problem Section

**Job:** Name the problem this product solves.

**Content requirements:**
- The frustration, gap, or question the product addresses
- Written in the audience's language
- Estimated words: 80-150

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance.
**Mobile:** Full-width text.

### 3. Key Takeaways

**Job:** Show what the reader/buyer will learn or get.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 bullet points of key outcomes or chapters
- Each bullet: specific and benefit-oriented
- Estimated words: 60-120

**CTA:** Secondary. "Read chapter 1 free" or "See the full table of contents".
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Bulleted list. No columns.

### 4. Author / Creator Credibility

**Job:** Explain why this person wrote/created this.

**Content requirements:**
- Brief bio focused on relevant expertise
- Connection between the creator's experience and the product's content
- Professional photo
- Estimated words: 50-100

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Credentials in copy.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Photo above bio text.

### 5. Sample Content

**Job:** Let them experience before they buy.

**Content requirements:**
- Excerpt, preview chapter, demo video, or sample pages
- Enough to demonstrate quality without giving away the whole product
- Estimated words: 100-300 (or embedded media)

**CTA:** Secondary. "Read more" or "Watch the full preview".
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "I can judge the quality myself."
**Mobile:** Embedded reader or video player. Full-width.

### 6. Reviews / Endorsements

**Job:** Let others sell for you.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 reviews from recognisable names or relatable peers
- Star ratings if from a platform (Amazon, Goodreads)
- Pull the most specific, result-oriented quotes
- Estimated words: 100-200

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Reviews and endorsements.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Swipeable review cards.

### 7. CTA Repeat

**Job:** Close the sale after proof has been presented.

**Content requirements:**
- Repeat primary CTA with fresh angle
- Price + what's included reminder
- Guarantee or risk reversal if applicable
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Primary. Same action as hero, different framing.
**Social proof:** Optional. Final endorsement quote.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Full-width CTA in thumb zone.

### 8. About the Author (Brief)

**Job:** Final credibility for visitors who scrolled the entire page.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 sentences. Professional photo.
- Link to full About page.
- Estimated words: 30-50

**CTA:** Secondary. Link to About page.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Compact layout. Photo beside text.

## Landing Page (Paid Traffic)

Stripped. No navigation. Single CTA. Message-matched to the ad that sent them here.
Every element exists to convert. Nothing else.

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Confirm they're in the right place. Mirror the ad headline.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: message-matched to ad copy (exact same promise)
- Subheadline: expand on the promise with specificity
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** None in hero text. Form or CTA comes in section 5.
**Social proof:** None yet.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance. "Yes, this is what I clicked for."
**Mobile:** Headline and subheadline only. No image needed.

### 2. Subheadline Expansion

**Job:** Add specificity to the promise.

**Content requirements:**
- 1-2 sentences expanding on the offer
- Specific: who it's for, what they get, how fast
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Part of the hero scroll.

### 3. Single Proof Signal

**Job:** One strong trust signal. Not a wall of testimonials.

**Content requirements:**
- One testimonial with a specific result, OR
- One metric ("4.9/5 from N reviews"), OR
- One logo bar of recognisable names
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** One strong signal. Quality over quantity.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Full-width. Compact.

### 4. Offer Explanation

**Job:** Explain what they get. Plain language.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 bullet points of what's included
- Each bullet: benefit-first, not feature-first
- Estimated words: 40-80

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Bulleted list. Full-width.

### 5. Form or CTA

**Job:** Capture the conversion.

**Content requirements:**
- Form: minimum fields (name + email for lead gen, or just email)
- Button: benefit-driven label (not "Submit")
- Friction reducer below button: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime." or guarantee
- Estimated words: 10-20 (plus form labels)

**CTA:** Primary. This is the only CTA on the page.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Distraction removal. Nothing competes.
**Mobile:** Form fields large. Button full-width. In thumb zone.

### 6. Supporting Proof

**Job:** One more trust signal for visitors who need extra reassurance.

**Content requirements:**
- One additional testimonial or data point
- Different angle from section 3 (address a different objection)
- Estimated words: 30-60

**CTA:** None. Or small text link repeating the primary action.
**Social proof:** Supporting testimonial.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Below the form. Compact.

### 7. Rules for This Page Type

- NO navigation. NO header links. NO footer links (except legal requirements).
- NO competing CTAs. One action only.
- NO links to other pages. Every click opportunity that is not the CTA is a leak.
- Message match: headline must mirror the ad that sent them here.
- Page load under 2 seconds. No heavy images. No video autoplay.

## Landing Page (Organic / SEO)

Same persuasion architecture as the homepage with additional content depth for search
ranking. Full navigation for crawling. Schema markup for rich results.

Follows the Homepage template (sections 1-10) with these modifications:

- **Full navigation** included (supports crawling and site exploration)
- **Content depth:** 1000-1500 words minimum (enough body text to rank)
- **Internal links:** 3-5 contextual links to related pages within body copy
- **Schema markup:** specify Organization, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList schemas
- **Meta description:** include a 150-160 character meta description in the blueprint
- **Header tag hierarchy:** one H1, logical H2/H3 nesting for crawler comprehension

## Blog / Resource Hub

Content discovery engine. Latest content featured. Categories for navigation. Newsletter
capture integrated.

### 1. Hero

**Job:** Establish the hub's purpose and set content expectations.

**Content requirements:**
- Hub title and brief description (what topics are covered, who it's for)
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** Optional. "Read by N+ [professionals/leaders]" if available.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance.
**Mobile:** Title and description only. Clean.

### 2. Featured / Latest Post

**Job:** Give the strongest content maximum visibility.

**Content requirements:**
- Large card: featured image, title, excerpt (2-3 sentences), date, read time
- One featured post. Not three. One.
- Estimated words: 30-50 (excerpt)

**CTA:** "Read this" or implicit (card is clickable).
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance + Clarity.
**Mobile:** Full-width card. Image above text.

### 3. Category Filters

**Job:** Help visitors find content relevant to their specific interest.

**Content requirements:**
- Topic/category pills or tabs
- 4-6 categories maximum. More creates decision paralysis.
- "All" as default active state
- Estimated words: 5-10 (category labels)

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Distraction removal. Narrow the options.
**Mobile:** Horizontal scroll pills.

### 4. Post Grid

**Job:** Display content for browsing.

**Content requirements:**
- Card layout: image, title, excerpt, date, read time, category tag
- 6-9 posts per page. Load more or pagination for archives.
- Sort by date (newest first) as default
- Estimated words: 15-25 per card (title + excerpt)

**CTA:** Implicit (cards are clickable).
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity.
**Mobile:** Single column. Cards full-width.

### 5. Newsletter Signup

**Job:** Convert readers into subscribers.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: what they get (not "Subscribe to our newsletter")
- Frequency and content promise ("Weekly insights on [topic]. No fluff.")
- Email field + button
- Estimated words: 20-40

**CTA:** Secondary. Email capture.
**Social proof:** Optional. Subscriber count if impressive.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. Clear expectations, easy unsubscribe.
**Mobile:** Full-width form. Large input and button.

### 6. Pillar Page Links

**Job:** Surface the deepest, most valuable content.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-3 pillar/cornerstone content links with descriptions
- Positioned as "Start here" or "Essential reading"
- Estimated words: 40-80

**CTA:** Secondary. Links to pillar content.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Relevance. Guide new visitors to the best content.
**Mobile:** Stacked cards with descriptions.

## Contact Page

Simple. Sets expectations. Multiple methods. No friction.

### 1. Headline

**Job:** Tell them what happens when they reach out.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: "Let's talk about [outcome]" or "Start a conversation"
- Subheadline: what to expect (response time, next steps)
- Estimated words: 15-30

**CTA:** None (the form IS the CTA).
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Clarity + Anxiety reduction.
**Mobile:** Centred text.

### 2. Contact Form

**Job:** Capture the enquiry with minimal friction.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 fields maximum: name, email, message. Optional: phone, company.
- Submit button with benefit label ("Send your question" not "Submit")
- Field labels clear. Placeholder text as guidance, not labels.
- Estimated words: 10-20 (labels and button)

**CTA:** Primary. Form submission.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Distraction removal. Nothing competes with the form.
**Mobile:** Full-width fields. Large touch targets. Button in thumb zone.

### 3. What to Expect

**Job:** Reduce anxiety about what happens after submission.

**Content requirements:**
- Response timeline: "You'll hear back within [timeframe]"
- What the first conversation looks like
- Estimated words: 30-60

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
**Conversion principle:** Anxiety reduction. "I know what happens next."
**Mobile:** Below the form. Compact text.

### 4. Alternative Contact Methods

**Job:** Provide options for people who prefer other channels.

**Content requirements:**
- Email address (clickable mailto link)
- Phone number (if applicable, clickable tel link)
- Social media links (if appropriate)
- Physical address (if relevant)
- Estimated words: 15-30

**CTA:** None.
**Social proof:** None.
