# Voice Profile — Got Moles

## Brand Voice Summary

Got Moles sounds like a straight-talking local expert who genuinely cares about solving your problem. Not corporate. Not salesy. The voice of a specialist who's seen it all, knows exactly what to do, and wants you to stop worrying. Spencer Hill's natural voice — confident, warm, community-rooted, and results-focused.

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use
- Specialist, not "service provider"
- Protect, restore, peace of mind
- Investigate, confirm, execute
- Evidence-based, custom strategy
- Year-round protection, ongoing control
- Chemical-free, safe for pets and children
- Locally rooted, community
- Results, guaranteed
- "We do one thing, and we do it better than anyone else"
- "We don't guess"

### Words We Never Use
- "Pest control" (we are mole specialists, not a pest company)
- "Extermination services" in a clinical/corporate way
- "Solutions" (vague corporate speak)
- "Leverage", "synergy", "cutting-edge" (corporate jargon)
- "Affordable" or "cheap" (we compete on expertise, not price)
- "Try" or "attempt" (we deliver results)
- Anything that sounds like a franchise or chain operation
- Technical trap names or methodology details (proprietary)

## Sentence Rhythm

Spencer's natural voice follows a pattern:
1. **Short declarative statement** — sets the truth
2. **Expansion** — one sentence of context
3. **Landing line** — drives it home

Example: "We do one thing, and we do it better than anyone else: mole extermination. That focus is our superpower. While other companies are spraying bugs, chasing rodents, or offering every service under the sun, we've chosen to specialize."

## Messaging Hierarchy

1. **Lead with empathy** — acknowledge the frustration before selling
2. **Establish specificity** — "we only do moles" is the instant differentiator
3. **Prove it** — numbers, reviews, the guarantee
4. **Offer the path forward** — clear next step, no friction

## Platform Rules

### Website Copy
- BLUF (Bottom Line Up First) on every page — the first 2-3 sentences must be a standalone, quotable answer
- Pain-first messaging: lead with their frustration, not the service description
- Outcome language: customers want their lawn back, not "trapping services"
- Fact density: a stat or proof point every 150-200 words
- Every page gets one primary CTA — never leave the visitor wondering what to do next

### Google Ads / Landing Pages
- Mirror the ad copy exactly in the headline
- Click-to-call above the fold
- Three trust signals immediately visible
- No navigation — keep them on the conversion path
- Guarantee statement in one line

### Social / Content
- Spencer's personal voice — first person, story-led
- Photos of real work, real yards, real results
- Community tone — "we live and work here too"

## Voice Don'ts

- Never sound like a general pest control company
- Never use vague claims without proof ("we're the best in the area" needs substantiation)
- Never use technical jargon about trap types or methodology
- Never hard-sell or use pushy pricing language
- Never reveal proprietary TMCP methodology details
- Never reference residential pricing on commercial materials or vice versa
