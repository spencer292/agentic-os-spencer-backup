# Got Moles? — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

Got Moles? is a veteran-owned mole control company that specializes exclusively in permanent mole control. The company does not offer general pest services or one-time fixes. It operates via a subscription-based Total Mole Control Program to eliminate current damage and prevent recurrence.

**Core Brand Values:** Reliable | Expert-Led | Results-Driven

### Color Usage Rules
- **Grass background:** Use Cream or Gold text/elements
- **Cream background:** Any color can be used
- **Blue background:** Use Cream or Gold text/elements

- **Rust background:** Use Cream or Gold text/elements

### Color Proportions
- Primary (Grass): ~60% of design space
- Secondary (Blue): ~30% of design space
- Accent (Gold, Rust): ~10% of design space

## Typography

### Primary Typeface — Lexend
- **Usage:** Headers and titles
- **Style:** Bold weight for headings

### Secondary Typeface — Zilla Slab SemiBold
- **Usage:** Subheadings
- **Style:** SemiBold weight; clean and legible; complements the logo

### Supporting Typeface — Zilla Slab Regular
- **Usage:** Body copy
- **Style:** Regular weight

### Typography Hierarchy
| Level      | Font                        | Weight      |
|------------|-----------------------------|-------------|
| Heading    | Lexend / Acumin Variable    | Bold        |
| Subheading | Zilla Slab                  | SemiBold    |
| Body Copy  | Zilla Slab                  | Regular     |

### Typography Rules — Do Not
- Do not use all capitals for long passages of text
- Do not add letter spacing to text
- Do not use too much or too little line spacing
- Do not place text too close to edges of page or design elements
- Do not use the same font size for headings, subheadings, and body copy
- Do not use inconsistent alignment within the same document

## Logo Suite

### Primary Logo
- **Usage:** Default logo; use most frequently when space allows. Suitable for websites, footers, stationery, signage.
- **Clear Space:** Minimum clear space equal to the height of the logo on all sides.
- **Appearance:** "GOT MOLES?" in bold display type with a distressed/earthy texture treatment.

### Secondary Logo
- **Usage:** When the primary logo does not fit the required space (e.g. websites, printed materials, larger elements). Stacked two-line layout: "GOT" on first line, "MOLES?" on second.
- **Clear Space:** Minimum clear space equal to half the height of the logo on all sides.

### Logo Mark
- **Usage:** Simplified icon for confined spaces — social media profile pictures, stamps, favicons. Use when other logo variations won't fit.
- **Appearance:** A stylised mole hill / earth mound silhouette.
- **Clear Space:** Minimum clear space of 0.5x the height of the logo mark.

---

## Logo — Incorrect Usage

The logo must never be altered. The following are prohibited:

- Do not alter the logo's colors in any way
- Do not lock up additional text to the logo
- Do not add elements, strokes, or drop shadows to the logo
- Do not place the logo inside a holding shape or box
- Do not alter the logo's shape in any way
- Do not rotate the logo
- Do not extend or condense the logo
- Do not outline the logo

## Brand Assets

### Illustrations
The brand uses detailed line-art skull/skeletal illustrations of moles. These are used across multiple color backgrounds and can be used individually or as a repeating pattern.

**Usage:**
- As a brand pattern on backgrounds (do not place content over the pattern as it becomes cluttered)
- Individually as focal points or scattered design elements

**Apply brand assets to:**
- Social media posts
- Packaging
- Website
- Vehicle graphics
- Printed material
- Signage

---

## Brand Voice

The Got Moles? brand communicates with authority, directness, and confidence. It is results-focused and professional, reflecting the veteran-owned ethos and specialist expertise.

**Tone attributes:**
- Reliable and consistent
- Expert and specialist (moles only — no generalist positioning)
- Results-driven — outcomes, not effort
- Respectful of customers and their property
- Honest and transparent

---

## Brand Positioning

- **Tagline examples in use:** "We Will Get Them Out" / "Got Moles?"
- **Specialist positioning:** Mole control is the only focus — never position as general pest control
- **Subscription model messaging:** Total Mole Control Program — long-term, managed service, not a one-time fix
- **Veteran-owned:** This should be referenced where appropriate as a trust and credibility signal
