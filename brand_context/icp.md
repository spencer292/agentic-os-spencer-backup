# Ideal Customer Profile — Got Moles

## Segment 1: Primary — The Frustrated Homeowner

### Persona: Jennifer & Mike Thompson
**Location:** Sammamish, Washington. Suburban neighborhood, 1/4 to 1/2 acre lots.

### Demographics
- Jennifer (42) — remote marketing role, manages the household
- Mike (45) — regional manager, construction supply company
- Two kids (9 and 11), one golden retriever, large backyard
- Household income: ~$150,000/year
- Home value: $700K+
- Recently invested $15,000 in landscaping and lawn irrigation

### Psychographics
- Proud homeowners who care deeply about how their property looks and feels
- Value professional service — will pay for results, not the cheapest option
- Busy schedules — want trusted specialists who handle things with minimal oversight
- Frustrated by recurring mole damage despite trying DIY and general pest companies
- Looking for a clear solution, not another gamble
- Not price-sensitive, but want confidence the money is well spent

### What They Say
> "We've tried everything, but these damn moles keep coming back. I'm tired of patching holes and wasting weekends."
> "I want someone who knows what they're doing — I don't care if it costs a little more, I just want it handled."

### Pain Points
1. DIY traps don't work — they've spent money proving it
2. A general pest company came out but didn't solve it
3. The moles keep returning — they've been through this cycle before
4. Mole damage is destroying a lawn they've invested significantly in
5. No time to manage this themselves
6. Embarrassed or frustrated by the state of their yard
7. Not sure who to trust or what will actually work

### What They Want
- Fast, effective removal with guaranteed results
- A **specialist**, not a general pest control tech
- No more guessing, no more wasted time
- A clean yard their kids and dog can enjoy
- Peace of mind through a long-term solution, not just one visit

### Primary Objection
"Will this actually work this time?"

### Secondary Objection
"Is it safe for my kids and dog?"

### Decision Driver
Proof and guarantee — not just promises.

### Emotional State at Arrival
Frustrated, slightly defeated, wants someone to take the problem away.

### How They Find Got Moles
- Google search (local, intent-driven: "mole removal [city]", "mole control Washington")
- Neighbour referrals and word of mouth
- Google reviews (219+ five-star reviews)
- Nextdoor and local community groups
- AdWords (decision-stage, high intent)

### Programme Fit
- **Entry:** One-Month Eradication Programme — $450 (incl. $150 setup fee, guarantee applies)
- **Retention:** Total Mole Control Program — $100/month, 12-month minimum

## Segment 2: Secondary — The Property Manager / Centre of Influence

### Demographics
- Facilities manager, grounds manager, estate manager, or operations manager
- Manages one or more commercial properties, HOA communities, or estates
- Answerable to a client, landlord, board, or owner
- May need procurement/finance sign-off before committing

### Psychographics
- This is a **professional and financial problem**, not an emotional one
- Values reliability and accountability above all else
- Needs a contractor they can trust to show up and not embarrass them
- Wants documentation and reporting — not just "it's done"
- May manage multiple sites — a good relationship expands across their portfolio

### Pain Points
1. Mole damage reflects poorly on their professional performance
2. Previous pest contractors were unreliable or used generalist technicians
3. Mole activity creates trip hazards on public-facing or sports-use land
4. Need a contractor who works within their schedule and access requirements
5. Need documentation for their own clients or management
6. Problem recurs seasonally — need managed long-term solution, not a call-out

### What They Want
- A specialist, not a generalist pest company
- Annual contract with clear scope, visit schedule, and reporting
- Reliable attendance — no no-shows before events or deadlines
- Understanding of professional grounds standards
- Flexibility for large or irregular land areas
- Licensed, insured, professional presentation

### Decision Driver
Reliability, accountability, and confidence in the contractor.

### How They Find Got Moles
- Google search (commercial-specific terms)
- Referral from another contractor or industry network
- Spencer's direct referral program ("51st Dates" — 50 referral lunches/year)
- Word of mouth from other property managers

### Programme Fit
- **All commercial:** Quoted individually after site inspection. Annual contracts. No fixed pricing.

### Commercial Niches (Distinct Buyer Types)
1. **Property management companies** — managing developments, HOAs, commercial estates
2. **Sports clubs & facilities** — pitches, training grounds, safety concerns
3. **Lawn care & landscaping specialists** — subcontracting mole control to protect their own results
4. **Property developers** — pre-sale and show-home grounds presentation
5. **Local authorities & public spaces** — parks, cemeteries, recreation grounds (chemical-free critical)

## Messaging Rules by Segment

### Residential
- Lead with empathy — they're exhausted and frustrated
- Lead with specificity — "we only do moles" differentiates instantly
- The guarantee is a risk remover, not just a feature
- TMCP is the logical next step, not an upsell
- Pet-safe and child-safe messaging matters (two kids and a dog)
- Lawn investment protection is a strong angle

### Commercial
- Lead with professionalism and reliability — not price, not emotion
- Emphasise annual contract model — managed service, not a call-out
- Highlight specialist focus — they're not getting a generalist
- B2B language: scope of work, service agreement, reporting, portfolio management
- Never reference residential pricing
- Case studies (even anonymised) are powerful here

### Messaging to Avoid (Both Segments)
- Anything that sounds like a general pest company
- Vague claims without proof
- Technical jargon about trap types or methodology
- Hard sell or pushy pricing language
