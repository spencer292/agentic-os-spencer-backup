# Recurring Pricing Review — Got Moles

Computed from 8,716 invoices (24 mo). Unit prices = line-item totalPrice ÷ quantity.

## What people actually pay today

| Product | Billing events | Median | 2026 median | Distribution |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TMCP membership** | 3,156 | **$100/mo** | $100 | **86% pay ≤$100**; 9% $101–125; ~2% $126–150 |
| Annual service (installments) | 3,936 | **$89/mo** | $89 | 83% ≤$100 — legacy floor pricing |
| One-month service | 1,317 | $450 | $450 | 82% $351–500 |
| Quick Fix | 127 | $450 | $450 | 87% $351–500 |

## The finding

**The recurring base is priced at the floor of the official tier card.** Tiers are $100/$125/$150 by
acreage, but 86% of TMCP billing lands at ≤$100 — either acreage tiers aren't being applied at
sign-up, or the base skews small-parcel, or discounts are habitual. Meanwhile the one-time side
($450 median, 69% close rate) proves willingness to pay is NOT the constraint.

Every $10/mo of recurring ARPU on the current 597 active members ≈ **$72K/yr**, straight to the
margin line (zero extra visits).

| # | Move | Mechanics | Annualized impact (ESTIMATE) | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | **Enforce existing tiers on new members** | Acreage check at quote time (VA already asks property size); CSR script + quote templates default to correct tier | +15–25% new-member ARPU; $0 churn risk | None — it's the published price |
| B | New-member tier raise | $125 / $150 / $175 | +$25/mo per new member vs today's actual | Low — close rate has headroom |

| D | Annual prepay option | 12 months for the price of 11 | Cash-flow + retention lock | Cannibalizes ~8% of prepayer revenue |
| E | Neighbor bundle | Both adjacent properties −$15/mo each | Density: 2 stops, 1 drive — margin up even at discount | None; line item already exists |
| F | Sunset legacy $89 "annual" pricing | Migrate renewals to current TMCP tiers | Closes the $89→$100+ gap on ~half the recurring events | Handle with grandfathered-until-renewal framing |

**My recommendation:** A immediately (it's free and it's just compliance), B for all quotes starting
next week, E systematized into ads/route planning, C as a 50-member test cohort in September (post
summer, pre fall-season goodwill), F at natural renewal only. Skip D unless cash is wanted for hires.
