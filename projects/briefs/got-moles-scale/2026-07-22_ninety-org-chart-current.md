## Visionary — Spencer Hill (7 roles, full text)

### 1. VISION & STRATEGIC DIRECTION
Set and communicate 10-year target, 3-year picture, 1-year plan.
Define annual revenue and growth goals. Identify which markets to enter and when.
Determine strategic priorities each quarter (inform Rock selection).
Anticipate market shifts and position company ahead of trends.
Balance growth ambitions with operational reality.

**Measurable Outcomes:** Clear 3-year picture exists and is updated annually; quarterly Rocks align with long-term vision; team understands where we're going and why.

### 2. BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT & PARTNERSHIPS
Develop strategic referral partnerships (pest control, landscaping, real estate).
Build relationships with high-value potential clients. Network at industry events, chambers of commerce, veteran business groups. Represent Got Moles publicly and build personal brand.
Identify and pursue commercial opportunities (HOAs, municipalities, golf courses).
Cultivate relationships that open doors to new markets or customer segments.
Negotiate partnership terms and agreements.

**Measurable Outcomes:** X active referral partnerships generating Y leads per month; Z% of new business from partnerships (track in scorecard); speaking engagements or networking events attended quarterly; new revenue channels identified and tested.

### 3. MARKET POSITIONING & BRAND STRATEGY
Define and protect brand identity (veteran-owned excellence, systematic approach).
Set pricing philosophy and strategy (premium vs. value vs. competitive).
Determine competitive differentiation strategy. Monitor competitive landscape and adjust positioning. Set marketing strategy and budget allocation. Approve major marketing campaigns and messaging. Ensure all customer touchpoints reflect brand values. Create thought leadership content (if applicable).

**Measurable Outcomes:** Brand positioning clearly articulated and documented; pricing strategy defined and communicated to Operator; marketing budget allocated strategically; brand awareness measurably increasing in target markets.

### 4. INNOVATION & NEW OPPORTUNITIES
Identify new service offerings (like Total Mole Control Program).
Evaluate adjacent revenue streams or business models. Research new technologies, methods, or tools for competitive advantage. Assess acquisition or partnership opportunities.
Challenge assumptions about "how things are done". Bring ideas from outside industries that could apply. Decide what to test, what to scale, what to kill.

**Measurable Outcomes:** New revenue streams tested quarterly; at least one major innovation implemented annually; ROI on innovation investments tracked.

### 5. CULTURE, VALUES & TEAM LEADERSHIP
Articulate and reinforce core values. Set cultural tone and expectations.
Define "culture fit" for hiring (especially leadership roles). Have final say on executive-level hires. Celebrate wins and recognize team achievements. Model the behavior expected from the team. Ensure veteran-owned identity is authentic and meaningful.
Address culture issues that Operator escalates.

**Measurable Outcomes:** Core values documented and referenced regularly; team engagement and morale high (track in employee feedback); culture fit in hiring decisions measurably improves retention; leadership team aligned on values.

### 6. FINANCIAL STRATEGY
Set financial goals beyond revenue (profit margin, cash reserves, EBITDA).
Decide capital allocation (growth vs. savings vs. distributions). Approve major capital expenditures (vehicles, equipment over $X threshold). Determine when to take on debt or raise capital. Set financial guard rails (minimum reserves, max debt). Plan exit strategy if applicable (build to sell vs. lifestyle business). Balance growth investment with profitability.

**Measurable Outcomes:** Financial goals clearly defined and tracked; capital allocation decisions made strategically; cash reserves maintained at target levels; ROI on major investments tracked.

### 7. SUPPORT & EMPOWER THE OPERATOR
Weekly Level 10 meeting with Operator (non-negotiable). Make strategic decisions quickly when Operator needs them. Remove roadblocks (resources, authority, budget). Provide context and "why" behind strategic direction. Listen to operational reality and adjust strategy accordingly. Stay out of operations unless Operator escalates. Give Operator authority to make decisions in his domain. Constructive challenge on Operator's thinking when needed.

**Measurable Outcomes:** Weekly Level 10 meetings held consistently (track attendance); Operator reports feeling supported and empowered (quarterly check-in); strategic decisions made within 48 hours when needed; Operator's Rocks are on track (he has what he needs).
