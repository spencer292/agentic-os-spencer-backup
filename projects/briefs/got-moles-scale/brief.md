# Got Moles — Scale to $5–10M/yr

## Goal

Take Got Moles from the current ~$1M/yr push to **$5–10M/yr within 2–3 years at ~30% EBITDA**, with Cory running operations and Spencer as pure visionary. Hands-off for Spencer at the operating level; every system AI-augmented.

Target picture at $10M: ~$3M annual profit, Spencer out of daily ops, Cory at the helm with a real org under him, AI carrying the back office (routing, phones, marketing, CRM hygiene, reporting).

## Phase 0 — Full diagnostic (FIRST, before any plan)

No growth plan until the baseline is real. Pull from live systems, not memory:

- **Revenue:** trailing 24 months from Jobber — by month, by service line (TMCP recurring vs Quick Fix vs other), growth rate, seasonality curve
- **Clients:** active TMCP count, lapsed/churned count + churn rate, LTV by segment, reactivation pool size
- **Pipeline & conversion:** CallRail call volume → booked rate, quote → close rate, average ticket
- **Capacity:** jobs/week per tech, route density, utilization — how much revenue can current crew even carry
- **Leakage:** AR aging, uninvoiced work (known: Karen Porter #5007 ~$2.9k; TMCP #8030+ untagged), pricing consistency
- **CAC by channel:** Ads spend/results, GBP/organic volume, referral rate
- **Output:** one baseline model + explicit gap-to-$5M math (clients needed × ticket × churn), and the honest TAM read

## Strategic vectors to evaluate (Phase 1, after diagnostic)

Ranked only after the data is in. Candidates:

1. **Density & share in current metro** — ads rebuild, GBP/review velocity, referral engine, close-rate lift (phone AI + training already in flight)
2. **Pricing & mix** — TMCP penetration of one-time customers, Quick Fix ladder, annual prepay
3. **TAM question (the honest one):** mole-only in Western WA may cap below $5M. If so, the vectors that break the ceiling:
   - **Geographic expansion** — new branches/metros (Portland? Spokane?) run on the same AI ops stack
   - **Service-line adjacency** — voles/gophers/wildlife (DECISION NEEDED: brand is deliberately mole-only; expansion vs. sub-brand)
   - **Acquisition** — buy competitors' books/routes; route-density businesses roll up well
   - **Licensing/franchise** — *The Route* is literally the playbook; license the model + AI stack to operators in other territories (bridges to the side project)
4. **Org for hands-off:** Cory → GM track (what he owns today vs. at $5M), hiring ladder, and which roles AI replaces vs. augments
