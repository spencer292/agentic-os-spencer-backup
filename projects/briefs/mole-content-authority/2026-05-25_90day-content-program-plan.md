# Got Moles — 90-Day Content Program (v2) + Key-Page Keyword Updates

The reboot. Move from biology-trivia to a **buyer-journey pillar architecture** that makes people
*understand mole control is hard, recurring, and worth a pro* — while winning Google + AI citations.
Built on first-hand experience (our 5,000 jobs), topical clusters, freshness discipline, and original data.

---

## 1. What the 2026 research says (the rules we build to)

- **Experience is the #1 E-E-A-T signal (March 2026 core update).** First-hand photos/video, documented process, real job data, named byline win; generic research-summary content loses. → every post carries an experience signal.
- **Topical clusters > single deep asset** (~40% more traffic; ~20 interlinked articles = authority). Niche focus is our edge. → 6 pillars, hub-and-spoke (internal linking already shipped).
- **Freshness is decisive:** 20-30% click decay / 6 months; 6-12 month clock; **<30-day updates = 3.2× AI citations**; visible "2026" = ~+30% citations; refresh quarterly. → **refresh is half the program.**
- **AEO content shape:** answer-first BLUF (+115% citations), original data (citation magnet), Q-format H2s, tables/numbered steps, stat blocks, schema, visible 2026 date.
- **Cadence:** consistency > volume; 1-2 quality posts/week. **Daily mass-AI volume = scaled-content-abuse penalty (50-80% drops).** Editorial oversight on every page.
- **Original research/data = #1 authority lever**; local AI Overviews (38% of local-service queries) need concrete local data.
- **Goal = "recognition, not rankings"** — entity authority + earned citations (digital PR).
- **Skip llms.txt** — Google doesn't use it (confirmed dud). We have it; leave it, invest nowhere near it.

## 2. The reframe

Biology trivia ("do moles have eyes") ranks and gets cited but **doesn't move anyone toward hiring**.
Reposition it so even biology earns its keep — *"moles dig 20 inches deep, breed fast, and eat
earthworms (not just grubs) → that's why DIY fails and they keep coming back."* Grow the **Control
Reality** pillar (currently 1 DIY post + 1 seasonal) into the biggest pillar. Every post answers the
reader's question honestly AND advances the felt understanding that this is hard, recurring work.

## 5. Post standard (every new + refreshed post must hit these)

1. **Answer-first BLUF** (40-60 words under H1, in the query's terms).
2. **An experience signal** — real photo / a stat from our 5,000 jobs / documented process / Spencer byline.
3. **Q-format H2s** carrying secondary keywords.
4. **One extractable asset** — a table, numbered steps, or a stat block.
5. **A "why this makes moles hard to control" tie + a contextual money link** (R2, already template-enforced) + cornerstone link.
6. **Schema** (BlogPosting + FAQPage + Person) + **visible 2026 date** + `dateModified`.
7. **Humanizer ≥8.5**, US English, Posture A (no mechanism/kill/poison framing).

## 8. Scheduling / "in motion" (honest mechanism)

- **2/week cadence via a production queue**, not unattended bot publishing. The 2026 research is explicit: every page needs **editorial oversight**; mass-auto-published AI content is the one thing that draws a penalty.
- **`ops-blog-pipeline`** produces each post (→ `tool-humanizer` ≥8.5 → `str-internal-links` wire → `str-onpage-audit` verify → publish). Refreshes touch `blog-data.ts` → `--reseed-blogs` → push.
- Optional automation: **Windows Task Scheduler** can fire a twice-weekly *reminder/draft-generation* job (cloud `/schedule` can't reach local keys; `ops-cron` is Mac-only). Keep a human approve-before-publish gate.
- **Quarterly refresh** is a standing calendar item (the decay clock).
