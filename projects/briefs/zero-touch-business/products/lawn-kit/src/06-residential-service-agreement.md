**Option B — Flat Monthly Billing**

## 5. Rain, Growth & Skipped Visits

Lawn care runs on weather, and honest expectations up front prevent every argument later.

- **Rain delays:** Company does not mow saturated turf — it rips, ruts, and clumps, and it's unsafe on slopes. If your scheduled day is rained out, your visit moves to the next dry working day, which may push the whole route back a day or two.
- **Growth-based skips:** In a slow-growth stretch (mid-summer heat, drought), a lawn may not need cutting on its normal cycle. Company may skip a cut when mowing would scalp or stress the turf. **Under per-cut billing, a skipped cut is not charged. Under flat-monthly billing, skips are already built into the average** (Section 4) — the monthly rate does not drop for a skipped week.
- **Client-requested skips:** If Client asks to skip a scheduled cut, please give at least **{24} hours' notice**. Skipping lets the lawn get taller, so the next visit takes longer; after {2} consecutive skips, Company may bill the next cut at a higher rate as a reset/overgrowth cut.
- **Catch-up after a delay:** When a lawn comes back overgrown after rain or a skip, the first cut back may be done in stages or at a higher deck height to protect the turf, and may carry an overgrowth charge if it materially exceeds a normal cut.

## 6. Seasonal Transitions & Winterization

- **First cut of the season** may run longer and cost more than a maintenance cut (winter debris, tall first growth) — this is normal and, where it exceeds a standard visit, is quoted as a spring cleanup or overgrowth cut.
- **Leaf season** changes the job. Once leaves fall, a "mow" becomes a mow-plus-leaf-management task and the time climbs. Leaf handling beyond incidental mulching is scoped and billed as fall cleanup (see Exhibit A), not folded silently into the mowing rate.
- **Final cut / winterization:** Company will confirm the last mow of the season, a lower final-cut height where appropriate, and any add-ons (final leaf cleanup, bed cutback) in writing before the account goes dormant for winter.
- **Snow removal**, if offered, is a **separate service under its own agreement** — it is not part of this lawn Agreement.

## 7. Property Access & Gates

**Access method** (check one):

- [ ] Property is open — no gate, crew has clear access to all turf areas
- [ ] Gated yard — Client will leave the gate **unlocked** on the service day
- [ ] Gate code / lock combination: provided separately (not written in this Agreement)
- [ ] Client will be home to provide access

**Locked-gate / no-access policy:** If the crew arrives on the scheduled day and cannot reach an area to be mowed — locked gate, blocked access, a dog loose in the yard — Company will mow what it safely can and note the rest. **A locked gate or blocked back yard that prevents completing the scope may be billed as a full visit** (the crew and the route time were committed), and the un-mowed area is not carried free to the next visit. Keep gates unlocked on your day and this never comes up.

**Pets:** Client is responsible for removing pets from the service area and picking up pet waste before the visit (see Section 8). The crew will not enter a yard with a loose dog and will skip that area rather than risk it.

## 8. Yard Condition & Obstacles — Client's Responsibility

Mowers and trimmers are powerful and fast, and the crew moves quickly across your whole yard. To keep your property and our equipment safe, **before each visit the Client agrees to clear the lawn of:**

- Toys, hoses, extension cords, tools, furniture, and pet items
- **Pet waste** — the crew will not mow through it, and a mower will fling it; heavily soiled areas will be skipped
- Rocks, branches, and debris large enough to become a projectile

**Permanent obstacles must be marked by the Client** or they are not the Company's responsibility if struck: **irrigation/sprinkler heads, invisible-fence wires and boundary flags, septic caps and cleanouts, drainage covers, landscape lighting, buried cable, shallow tree roots, and stakes.** Company will mow and trim carefully around anything visibly marked; unmarked hazards are addressed in Section 9 and the separate Property Condition & Damage Waiver.

## 9. Damage, Liability & Property Condition

- Company will operate its equipment with reasonable care. If Company clearly damages an item through negligence, Client must report it within **{48} hours** of the visit. Company will, at its option, repair, replace with like kind and value, or credit the reasonable value.
- **Inherent-risk items:** String trimmers and mowers throw debris and can nick surfaces. Company is **not responsible** for damage to **unmarked** irrigation heads, invisible-fence wires, low-voltage lighting, shallow roots, or landscape edging, or for **bark/trunk damage to young or unprotected trees** where no trunk guard is installed. Client is responsible for marking these and protecting young trees (see the Property Condition & Damage Waiver, File 13).
- **Rock/object throw:** Mowers can eject small rocks, mulch, or objects at speed. Company clears what it reasonably sees, but is not liable for damage from objects hidden in turf or beds, or thrown from unmarked gravel areas. Windows, vehicles, and pets should be kept clear of the crew.
- **Pre-existing lawn conditions** — grubs, fungus, drought stress, thinning, weeds, moss, uneven ground, and prior damage — are **not** the Company's responsibility and are not "caused by mowing." Company will flag conditions it notices and can quote treatment separately.
- Except where prohibited by law, Company's total aggregate liability under this Agreement is limited to the **greater of the amount paid by Client in the {3} months preceding the claim or ${LIABILITY CAP}**. Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.
- Company carries general liability insurance: carrier {INSURER}, policy limits {LIMITS}. Certificate available on request.

## 10. Chemical Applications (Fertilizer & Weed Control)

If fertilization, weed control, or pest treatment is checked in Section 2, the following applies:

- **Licensing:** Applying fertilizers and pesticides for hire commonly requires a **state pesticide/fertilizer applicator license**. Company will perform these services only under a valid license held by Company or a licensed subcontractor. Where Company is not licensed for a requested treatment, it will say so and refer the Client out — it will not apply restricted products without the proper credential. *(Requirements vary by state — this Agreement makes no representation of compliance beyond holding the required license where one is used.)*
- **Results:** Turf and weed outcomes depend on weather, watering, soil, and timing. Company does not guarantee eradication of any weed, pest, or disease.
- **Re-entry & pets/children:** Client agrees to follow posted re-entry intervals and keep children and pets off treated areas until dry / for the interval stated on the product label.

Complaints raised after the notice window, or after grass has visibly grown since the visit, are handled case-by-case.
