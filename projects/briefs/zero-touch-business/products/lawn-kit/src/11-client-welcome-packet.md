## Before We Arrive — Three Things That Help (and One That Really Matters)

A two-minute walk of your yard before your service day keeps everything running smooth:

1. **Unlock the gate.** If we can't get into the back yard, we mow what we can reach and the rest waits — and a locked gate may still count as a full visit, because the crew and route time were committed. Leave it unlocked on your day and this never comes up.
2. **Clear the lawn.** Pick up toys, hoses, cords, tools, and **pet waste**. A mower will fling anything it hits, and it won't mow cleanly through pet waste — soiled areas get skipped.
3. **Keep pets inside on service day.** Our crew won't enter a yard with a loose dog. If Fido's out, that area gets skipped for everyone's safety.

**The one that really matters — mark your hidden stuff.** Before your first visit, please flag anything in the lawn we can't see: **sprinkler/irrigation heads, invisible-fence wires, septic caps, drainage covers, low-voltage lighting, and any young or newly planted trees.** We mow and trim carefully around anything marked. Unmarked items in the turf are, unfortunately, mowed at your own risk — there's no way for a crew moving across a whole yard to find what isn't flagged. (Full details are in the Property Condition & Damage Waiver we sent with your agreement.)

## Rain, Heat & Skipped Cuts — How Weather Works

Lawn care runs on weather, and being straight with you up front saves a lot of confusion later:

- **If it rains on your day, we push, not skip.** We don't mow saturated lawns — it ruts the turf and leaves clumps. Your visit moves to the next dry working day, which can bump the whole route a day or two. We'll catch you up.
- **In a slow-growth stretch** (mid-summer heat, drought), your lawn may not need cutting on its normal cycle. We may skip a cut rather than scalp stressed grass. **On per-cut billing, a skipped cut isn't charged. On flat-monthly billing, skips are already built into your rate** — busy months and slow months average out, so the monthly bill stays the same.
- **Coming back from a delay:** If your lawn bounces back tall after rain or a skip, we may cut it a little higher or in a couple of passes to protect it. A cut that's dramatically overgrown may carry a small reset charge — we'll tell you first.

## Leaf Season & Seasonal Transitions

- **The first cut of spring** often takes extra time (winter debris, tall growth). If it's really a cleanup, we'll quote it as one before we do it.
- **Once the leaves fall**, a "mow" becomes a mow-plus-leaves and the time climbs. Light mulching of leaves is part of a normal cut; full **leaf cleanup and removal is a seasonal add-on** — we'll offer it, not spring it on you.
- **End of season:** We'll confirm your final cut, a lower final height where it helps, and any fall cleanup before your account goes dormant for winter.
- **Snow removal**, if we offer it in your area, is a separate winter service with its own simple agreement — just ask.
