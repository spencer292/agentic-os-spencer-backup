# COMMERCIAL LANDSCAPE MAINTENANCE CONTRACT

## 1. Parties

This Commercial Landscape Maintenance Contract ("Contract") is entered into as of **{DATE}** by and between:

**Contractor:** {BUSINESS NAME}, a {ENTITY TYPE} organized under the laws of {STATE}, with its principal place of business at {BUSINESS ADDRESS} ("Contractor").

**Client:** {CLIENT LEGAL NAME — e.g., property management company / HOA association}, a {ENTITY TYPE}, with its principal place of business at {CLIENT ADDRESS} ("Client").

**Property / properties serviced:** {PROPERTY NAME(S) & ADDRESS(ES)} (the "Property"), approximately **{TOTAL TURF AREA / ACREAGE}** of maintained landscape.

## 2. Services

Contractor will perform landscape maintenance at the Property as described in **Exhibit A — Scope of Work & Seasonal Frequency Schedule**, which is incorporated into this Contract. Services not listed in Exhibit A are out of scope and will be quoted separately as change orders or one-time projects.

Contractor will supply trained personnel, supervision, equipment, and management of the work. Contractor is an independent contractor; nothing in this Contract creates an employment, agency, or joint-venture relationship.

## 3. Season Schedule & Site Access

- **Service window:** Landscape maintenance is performed on a **seasonal schedule** (Exhibit A), with mowing frequency stepping up in peak growth (spring/early summer) and down in slow growth (mid-summer, dormancy). Frequencies in Exhibit A are the baseline; Contractor adjusts within them for weather and growth.
- **Service days:** {DAYS} between {START TIME} and {END TIME}. Contractor routes by area and may vary the exact time of day.
- **Weather:** Contractor will not mow saturated turf or operate in unsafe conditions (lightning, high wind, flooding). Weather-delayed visits shift to the next working day; the seasonal scope is delivered across the season, not guaranteed on a fixed calendar day.
- **Access & security:** Client will provide {GATE CODES / ACCESS CARDS / KEYS — QUANTITY} where areas are secured. Contractor will restrict credentials to assigned staff, secure gates on exit, and report any signs of damage or forced entry to {CLIENT CONTACT — NAME / PHONE}.
- **Coordination:** Client will identify parking areas to avoid during business hours, tenant-sensitive zones, and any areas requiring escorted access.

## 5. Pricing

- **Recurring maintenance fee:** **${MONTHLY FEE} per month**, billed evenly across the {N — e.g., 12}-month term, covering the seasonal scope and frequencies in Exhibit A. *(Even monthly billing across the year is standard for commercial accounts — it smooths the client's budget and yours, even though the field work is heavier in growing season.)*
- Alternatively: **${ANNUAL FEE} per year**, invoiced {MONTHLY / QUARTERLY}.
- **One-time / project work** (mulch refresh, aeration/overseeding, seasonal color, irrigation repair, storm cleanup, tree work): quoted and approved in writing before performance.
- **Annual escalator:** On each anniversary of the Effective Date, the recurring fee will increase by **{3}%** (or by the change in CPI-U for {REGION}, if greater, capped at {5}%). Contractor will confirm the adjusted fee in writing at least {30} days before it takes effect. *(Fuel, wages, and equipment costs rise every year; a built-in escalator is normal on multi-year landscape contracts and prevents an awkward renegotiation.)*
- **Scope changes:** If the Property's maintained area, plant beds, or frequency needs change materially, either party may request a re-quote; the adjusted fee applies after written agreement.

## 6. Invoicing & Payment

- Contractor invoices **{MONTHLY IN ADVANCE / MONTHLY IN ARREARS}** on or about the {1st} of each month.
- **Payment terms** (check one): [ ] **Net 15**  [ ] **Net 30**  [ ] **Net 45** from invoice date.
- Accepted methods: {ACH / CHECK / CARD (note any card surcharge, where permitted by law)}.
- Overdue balances accrue a late charge of {1.5}% per month (or the maximum permitted by law, if lower). If any invoice is more than **{30} days** past due, Contractor may suspend service on {5} business days' written notice until the account is current; suspension does not extend or terminate the Contract.

## 7. Materials, Equipment & Water/Power

- **Equipment and standard maintenance materials** (fuel, trimmer line, blades) are provided by Contractor and included in the recurring fee.
- **Bulk materials** (mulch, soil, seed, sod, plant material, chemical products) are billed at cost plus {10–15}% when supplied by Contractor, itemized and approved in advance.
- Client provides reasonable access to a **water source** for any watering-in of installs and, where applicable, **power** for equipment; Client is responsible for its own irrigation system operation and repair unless separately contracted.
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for any products used are available on request.

## 8. Quality Standards, Inspections & Cure Period

- Contractor will perform services in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards for comparable properties.
- **Inspections:** Contractor's supervisor will walk the Property at least **{monthly}** and share a brief report or checklist with {CLIENT CONTACT}. Client may join any inspection or request a walkthrough.
- **Deficiency & cure:** If Client identifies a service deficiency, Client will notify Contractor in writing (email acceptable) with reasonable detail. Contractor will correct it within **{2} business days** ("Cure Period") at no additional charge.
- **Persistent failure:** If the same documented deficiency recurs {3} or more times in any {60}-day period after written notice, Client may terminate for cause on {10} days' written notice without the termination-for-convenience notice period in Section 10.

## 9. Term & Renewal

- **Initial term:** **{12} months** beginning {START DATE} (the "Effective Date").
- **Renewal:** This Contract automatically renews for successive **{12}-month** terms unless either party gives written non-renewal notice at least **{60} days** before the end of the then-current term.
- The annual escalator in Section 5 applies at each renewal.

## 10. Termination

- **For convenience:** Either party may terminate this Contract without cause on **{30 / 60} days' written notice**.
- **For cause:** Either party may terminate on {10} days' written notice if the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within the notice period; non-payment beyond {30} days and the persistent-failure condition in Section 8 are material breaches.
- **On termination:** Client pays for all services performed through the termination date; Contractor returns all keys, cards, and Client property and removes its equipment within {5} business days.

## 11. Liability, Indemnification & Property Condition

- Each party will indemnify the other against third-party claims for bodily injury or property damage to the extent caused by its own negligence or willful misconduct.
- Contractor will promptly report any damage its staff causes and will repair, replace, or credit at its option. Claims must be reported by Client within {5} business days of discovery.
- **Landscape-specific exclusions:** Contractor is not responsible for damage to **unmarked** irrigation heads, valve boxes, low-voltage lighting, or buried utilities/cable; for pre-existing turf conditions (disease, insect damage, drought stress, thin or bare areas); or for plant loss due to weather, watering failures outside Contractor's control, or existing plant health. Contractor will flag such conditions and can quote remediation.
- Except for indemnification obligations, insurance proceeds, and confidentiality breaches, neither party's aggregate liability under this Contract will exceed the **fees paid or payable in the {6} months preceding the claim**, and neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages (including lost profits or business interruption).

## 12. Chemical Applications & Licensing

If Exhibit A includes fertilization, weed control, or pest treatment:

- Contractor represents that all such applications will be performed by a **licensed applicator** (Contractor's or a licensed subcontractor's) holding the credential required by {STATE} for the products used, and will maintain records of applications as required.
- Contractor will post/notify per applicable regulation and observe label re-entry intervals. Contractor makes no guarantee of eradication; outcomes depend on weather, irrigation, and site conditions.
- This Section states the licensing basis for the work; it is not a representation of compliance with every applicable regulation, which Client acknowledges varies by jurisdiction.

## 13. Personnel & Non-Solicitation

- Contractor is responsible for hiring, screening ({BACKGROUND CHECK POLICY}), training, paying, and supervising its staff, including payroll taxes and workers' compensation.
- Client may request removal of any Contractor employee from its account for reasonable cause, and Contractor will replace that employee promptly.
- During the term and for **{12} months** after, neither party will solicit for employment the other's personnel who worked under this Contract, without written consent. A party that does agrees to pay a placement fee of **${PLACEMENT FEE — e.g., 5,000}**.

## 14. General Terms

- **Force majeure:** Neither party is liable for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable control (severe weather, drought watering restrictions, government order); the fee is equitably adjusted for extended interruptions.
- **Assignment:** Neither party may assign this Contract without the other's written consent, except to a successor in a sale of substantially all its business.
- **Notices:** Written notices go to the contacts below by email with confirmation, or by certified mail.
- **Entire agreement / amendments:** This Contract with its Exhibits is the entire agreement; amendments must be in writing and signed (or confirmed by email).
- **Severability & waiver:** Unenforceable provisions are severed; a waiver of one breach is not a waiver of others.
- **Governing law:** The laws of the State of {STATE} govern this Contract.
