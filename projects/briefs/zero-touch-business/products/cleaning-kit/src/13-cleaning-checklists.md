# Cleaning Business Starter Kit
**File 13 — Cleaning Checklists (Standard / Deep / Move-Out)** | v1.0 | 2026-07-19

---

## How to use this

Print these, laminate them, put them in every crew bag. A checklist is not an insult to a good cleaner — it's how a good cleaner proves the job is done and how you deliver the same clean whether it's you, your best tech, or a new hire on day three. Every callback you ever get will trace to a line someone skipped.

The order matters. Each room runs **top to bottom** (dust falls — never clean low before high), the house runs **back to front** (finish at the door, never walk back through finished rooms), and surfaces go **dry to wet** (dust and vacuum before you spray and wipe, so you're not making mud). Start with the deepest room from the entry and work your way out.

---

## CREW STANDARDS — Every Job, Every Time

1. **Arrival:** Arrive inside the window. Text the client (or office) on arrival and on completion.
2. **Shoes:** Shoe covers or dedicated indoor shoes on at the door — no exceptions, no "just this once."
3. **Photos:** Photograph any pre-existing damage BEFORE you start. If you didn't photograph it, you own it.
4. **Supplies:** Your kit, your labeled bottles, correct product for the surface. Never use an unlabeled or client chemical without a note on file.
5. **Lock-up:** Doors locked, alarm set, lights and thermostat per client instructions, key/code secured. Never let a pet out.
6. **Report:** Final walkthrough against this list, note anything skipped or found (damage, low supplies, concerns), send completion report before driving away.

# CHECKLIST 1 — STANDARD CLEAN

*The recurring visit. This is the product your clients are paying for every week or two — it should look identical no matter who's on the crew.*

## All Rooms — Basics (applies in every room as you go)

- [ ] High dust: corners, ceiling fans (tops), vents, door frames
- [ ] Dust light fixtures and lamps (dry)
- [ ] Dust wall hangings, ledges, sills, and blinds (dry)
- [ ] Dust furniture and decor top-down; lift and wipe under items, don't dust around them
- [ ] Spot-clean light switches, door handles, and door faces
- [ ] Spot-clean visible marks on walls (test surface first)
- [ ] Empty trash, wipe lid if needed, reline can
- [ ] Vacuum all floors and edges (dry step); hard floors then mopped (wet step, working toward the room's exit)
- [ ] Final look from the doorway: straighten pillows, square rugs, done

## Kitchen

- [ ] Top of refrigerator and upper cabinet faces — dust, then damp-wipe
- [ ] Range hood exterior and control panel
- [ ] Cabinet and drawer faces — spot-wipe fingerprints and drips
- [ ] Microwave — inside and out, including turntable
- [ ] Stovetop, burners/grates, and control knobs — degrease
- [ ] Small appliances — wipe exterior (coffee maker, toaster — crumb tray dumped)
- [ ] Countertops and backsplash — clear, wipe, replace items neatly
- [ ] Sink and faucet — scrub, de-spot, polish dry (chrome shines = clean reads)
- [ ] Exterior of refrigerator, dishwasher, oven — including handles
- [ ] Table and chairs — wipe tops, seats, and rungs
- [ ] Trash and recycling out; floors last: vacuum, then mop backing out of the room

## Bathrooms

- [ ] High dust: vent fan cover, light bar, top of mirror
- [ ] Apply bowl cleaner to toilet FIRST — let it dwell while you clean the rest
- [ ] Mirror — glass-clean, streak-free
- [ ] Counter, sink, and faucet — clear, scrub, wipe, polish chrome dry
- [ ] Shower/tub — walls top-down, fixtures, soap dish, door or curtain check; rinse and dry-wipe glass
- [ ] Toilet — tank, lid, seat (both sides), rim, bowl scrub, base, and the floor behind it
- [ ] Towels straightened or replaced per client instruction
- [ ] Empty trash, reline
- [ ] Floor — vacuum, then mop, backing out the door

## Bedrooms

- [ ] High dust and ceiling fan
- [ ] Make beds; change linens if client left fresh sets out
- [ ] Dust all furniture top-down: headboard, dressers, nightstands, lamps
- [ ] Mirrors — glass-clean
- [ ] Wipe switches and handles
- [ ] Empty trash
- [ ] Vacuum floor including under-bed edge as far as the vacuum reaches; mop if hard floor

## Living Areas (living room, dining, office, hallways, entry)

- [ ] High dust, fans, and vents
- [ ] Dust electronics and TV screen (dry microfiber only — never spray a screen)
- [ ] Dust shelves, decor, and furniture top-down
- [ ] Glass surfaces and interior glass doors — streak-free
- [ ] Straighten cushions, fold throws; vacuum upholstery surfaces and under cushions (weekly rotation is fine)
- [ ] Wipe stair rail and spindles on the way down
- [ ] Vacuum all floors, edges, and stairs; mop hard floors, finishing at the front door

# CHECKLIST 2 — DEEP CLEAN

*Everything in the STANDARD clean above, PLUS the items below. Quote deep cleans at roughly 1.5–2x a standard visit — this is where the time goes. A deep clean is also the correct first visit for every new recurring client: you're buying the baseline that standard visits will maintain.*

## All Rooms — Deep Additions

- [ ] Ceiling fan blades — both sides, washed not just dusted
- [ ] Light fixtures — globes removed and washed where practical
- [ ] Vents and returns — vacuumed and washed
- [ ] Blinds — slat-by-slat damp wipe; window sills and tracks detailed
- [ ] Window interiors — glass cleaned
- [ ] Baseboards — full hand-wipe, every room
- [ ] Doors, frames, and switch plates — full wash, both sides of doors
- [ ] Walls — spot-wash reachable scuffs and marks throughout
- [ ] Move light furniture and clean beneath/behind

## Kitchen — Deep Additions

- [ ] Inside oven (full degrease — check self-clean instructions first)
- [ ] Inside refrigerator — shelves and drawers out and washed
- [ ] Cabinet and drawer faces — full wash, not just spot-wipe, including hardware
- [ ] Backsplash — full degrease, grout lines scrubbed
- [ ] Under and behind small appliances
- [ ] Pull refrigerator forward (if safely movable) — clean sides, top, and floor beneath
- [ ] Dishwasher — filter cleaned, door edges and gasket wiped
- [ ] Trash can — washed inside and out, deodorized

## Bathrooms — Deep Additions

- [ ] Tile grout — scrubbed (shower walls and floor)
- [ ] Shower door tracks and frame — detailed
- [ ] Showerhead — descaled (bag-and-vinegar soak or per product)
- [ ] Cabinet faces and hardware — full wash
- [ ] Behind the toilet and around the base — hand-detail, including bolt caps
- [ ] Exhaust fan cover — removed and washed
- [ ] Drains — visible buildup cleared at stopper

## Bedrooms & Living Areas — Deep Additions

- [ ] Under beds and large furniture — vacuumed fully (move what two people can safely move)
- [ ] Mattress surface vacuumed; bed frame and headboard detailed
- [ ] Upholstery — full vacuum including backs and skirts
- [ ] Lamp shades — dusted/vacuumed
- [ ] Closet floors — vacuumed; shelves dusted where accessible
- [ ] Electronics and cords — dusted carefully; media console interior wiped

# CHECKLIST 3 — MOVE-OUT CLEAN

*Everything in the DEEP clean above, PLUS the empty-home additions below. The home should be empty of belongings before you start — put that in the quote. The standard here is "walkthrough-ready": a landlord, buyer, or property manager will inspect this work looking for reasons to withhold a deposit. Photograph every room when you finish — your photos protect your client AND you.*

## Empty-Home Additions

- [ ] Inside ALL cabinets and drawers — every room: kitchen, baths, laundry, built-ins — vacuum crumbs, then wash
- [ ] Inside all closets — shelves wiped, rods wiped, floors vacuumed, corners de-cobwebbed
- [ ] Inside all appliances that remain: oven, refrigerator/freezer (turned off, propped open when done), dishwasher, microwave, washer/dryer (drums, gaskets, dispensers, lint trap)
- [ ] Walls — full wash where washable; magic-eraser scuffs; nail holes noted for client (patching is usually not your job — say so in the quote)
- [ ] All baseboards, trim, and crown — full wipe
- [ ] All doors both sides, frames, closet doors and tracks
- [ ] All window interiors, sills, tracks, and latches
- [ ] Light fixtures and fans — fully washed; replace obvious dead bulbs if client provided them
- [ ] Vents, returns, and reachable ductwork faces
- [ ] Behind and beneath every appliance that moves
- [ ] Water heater closet / utility areas — swept and wiped
- [ ] Garage — cobwebs down, shelving wiped, floor swept (blown or broom); note oil stains (degreasing is an add-on)
- [ ] Entry/porch — sweep, cobwebs, wipe front door and hardware
- [ ] Final pass: every floor vacuumed then mopped, backing out the front door; lights off; photos of every room; lock up
