## How to use this

Four tools in one file, and each one exists because a real problem will eventually walk through your door:

- **(a) Weather & Rescheduling Policy** — pressure washing lives and dies by weather. Put this in your welcome packet from day one so a rained-out date is "our policy," not a fight.
- **(b) Cancellation & No-Show Policy** — a policy announced *after* the first no-show feels like punishment; announced up front, it's just how you do business.
- **(c) Price-Increase Letter** — you'll need this sooner than you think. Raise recurring prices annually and modestly instead of rarely and painfully.
- **(d) Review-Request & Complaint Scripts** — copy, personalize, send within hours. For this trade, ask for the review with an after photo attached; it's your best marketing asset.

Replace every `{PLACEHOLDER}`. These are business templates, not legal documents — fee limits and notice requirements vary by state/county, so verify locally before enforcing fees.

# (a) Weather & Rain Reschedule Policy

*Insert into your service agreement and welcome packet.*

---

## {BUSINESS NAME} — Weather & Rescheduling Policy

Pressure washing is outdoor work, and doing it right depends on the weather. We won't wash in conditions that hurt the result or anyone's safety.

**When we reschedule.** We'll move your appointment for:
- **Steady or heavy rain** — soft-wash cleaning solutions need time to dwell and a controlled rinse; rain washes them off before they've worked.
- **High wind** — it scatters overspray onto cars, neighbors, and plants we're trying to protect.
- **Freezing temperatures** — water and equipment don't cooperate, and wet surfaces ice over.
- **Lightning / storms** — we don't run equipment or climb in it.

**How it works.** If the forecast forces a change, we'll contact you as early as we can and move you to the **next available date** — no penalty, no lost deposit. Weather reschedules are common in this line of work; we'd rather do it right next week than poorly today.

**Light rain after we finish** is nothing to worry about — it won't undo the clean. In fact, clean rain on a freshly washed surface is a free rinse.

**Your flexibility helps.** During busy or rainy stretches, having a backup day or two open makes it much easier for us to fit you back in quickly.

Questions? {PHONE / EMAIL}.

## {BUSINESS NAME} — Cancellation, Lockout & No-Access Policy

Your appointment reserves a slot on our schedule and a paycheck for our crew. When a visit disappears at the last minute, that time is almost never re-bookable. This policy keeps things fair for everyone.

**Notice window.** We ask for at least **{48} hours' notice** to cancel or reschedule. Reach us at {PHONE / EMAIL} anytime — voicemails and texts sent before the window count as notice. (Weather reschedules **we** initiate are always free — see our Weather Policy.)

**Late cancellation.** Cancellations made with less than {48} hours' notice are charged **{$XX / X% of the job}**.

**Lockout / no-access.** If our crew arrives during your scheduled window and can't do the work — **locked gate, blocked driveway or work area, no working water spigot, or pets loose in the yard** — the visit is charged as a trip fee at **{$XX}**. We'll try to reach you before leaving. A quick prep check the morning of (gate open, spigot on, area clear, pets in) prevents this every time.

**Repeat short-notice cancellations.** Life happens — once. If {3} or more visits in a rolling {12}-month period are canceled with short notice, we may move you from a reserved recurring slot to flexible scheduling, or ask for prepayment to hold your date. We'll always talk with you first.

**Our side of the deal.** If *we* ever cancel on you with short notice for a non-weather reason, your next visit is discounted {X%}. The policy cuts both ways.

Questions? Ask before you need it: {PHONE / EMAIL}.
