## 6. Pets, Children & the Work Zone

Pressure washing involves high-pressure spray, chemical cleaning solutions, wet slippery surfaces, and hoses across walkways. For everyone's safety:

- Client agrees to keep **pets indoors** and **children out of the immediate work area** during service.
- Client agrees to keep the family off freshly washed hard surfaces until they have dried and any cleaning solution has been rinsed and dissipated — wet concrete and decking are slip hazards.
- Client will point out any pet water bowls, garden beds, koi ponds, or outdoor pet areas so the crew can protect them.

## 7. Landscaping, Plants & Overspray

Soft-wash cleaning solutions and rinse water can affect plants if not managed. Company will take reasonable precautions — pre-wetting and post-rinsing vegetation near the work, and controlling overspray where practical.

- Client agrees to move or identify any prized, delicate, or newly planted vegetation before the crew arrives.
- Client will close windows and doors, and remove or identify items on affected walls/eaves (hanging baskets, flags, cameras, light fixtures, satellite dishes) that could be reached by spray.
- Company is not responsible for incidental effects to landscaping despite reasonable precautions, or for damage to items Client was asked to move or secure and did not.

## 8. Pre-Existing Conditions & Surface-Damage Risk (read carefully)

Exterior surfaces age, and cleaning reveals — and can occasionally aggravate — conditions that were already present. Client understands and agrees:

- **Pre-existing damage is documented, not created.** Before starting, the crew will note and, where practical, photograph conditions such as **oxidation/chalking on siding, loose or peeling paint, failed or missing caulk and window seals, cracked or spalling concrete, loose mortar, wood rot or soft boards, rusted fasteners, damaged screens, and lifted or curling shingles**. These conditions exist independent of Company's work.
- **Cleaning can expose or worsen weak surfaces.** Washing can loosen already-failing paint, remove oxidation unevenly on faded/chalky siding (leaving a "cleaner" look that highlights age), fuzz or raise the grain on older wood, and reveal cracks or stains that dirt was hiding. On roofs and screens, force is kept low precisely to avoid damage, but brittle, aged, or improperly installed materials can still react.
- **Water intrusion via existing gaps.** Water can enter through pre-existing failures in window seals, siding gaps, door sweeps, and worn caulk. Client is responsible for the condition of these seals; Company is not liable for intrusion through defects that existed before service.
- **Stains and limitations.** Rust, oil/grease, paint, irrigation (mineral) stains, tannin bleed, artillery fungus, and deep organic staining **may not fully lift** even with appropriate treatment. Company will manage expectations before starting and does not guarantee complete removal of these.

Client's signature on this Agreement acknowledges these risks. See also the separate **Liability Waiver & Property Condition Acknowledgment** (File 13), which Company may ask Client to sign for jobs involving older or higher-risk surfaces.
