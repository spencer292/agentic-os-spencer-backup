## 7. Water, Chemicals & Runoff Compliance

- **Water:** per Exhibit A ({Client spigot / Contractor tank}).
- **Cleaning agents:** Contractor uses commercial-grade detergents and, for organic growth, sodium-hypochlorite-based soft-wash solutions applied at appropriate dilution; Safety Data Sheets (SDS) available on request.
- **Runoff:** Contractor follows reasonable best-management practices to contain, divert, and dilute wash water. **Local wastewater/stormwater discharge rules vary by jurisdiction** and can be strict for commercial sites (food service, automotive, fuel). Client and Contractor each acknowledge responsibility to be aware of the rules applicable to the Premises; Contractor makes no regulatory-compliance guarantee, and any site-specific containment/reclamation the Premises requires beyond standard practice is priced as an add-on.

## 8. Quality, Inspections & Cure

- Contractor performs services in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.
- **Deficiency & cure:** on written notice (email acceptable) with reasonable detail, Contractor will re-service the affected area within **{2} business days** at no additional charge — excluding stains and conditions disclosed as not fully removable and normal re-soiling.
- **Persistent failure:** if the same documented deficiency recurs {3}+ times in any {60}-day period after notice, Client may terminate for cause on {10} days' notice.

## 9. Weather & Force Majeure

Exterior work is weather-dependent. Contractor will not wash in unsafe conditions (rain preventing proper dwell/rinse, high wind, freezing temperatures, lightning) and will reschedule affected visits to the next available date without penalty. Neither party is liable for delays caused by events beyond reasonable control; recurring fees are equitably adjusted for extended interruptions.

## 10. Term, Renewal & Termination

- **Initial term:** **{12} months** from {START DATE} (the "Effective Date").
- **Renewal:** automatically renews for successive **{12}-month** terms unless either party gives written non-renewal notice at least **{60} days** before term end.
- **Termination for convenience:** either party on **{30} days' written notice**.
- **For cause:** on {10} days' notice for uncured material breach (non-payment beyond {30} days and the persistent-failure condition are material breaches).
- **On termination:** Client pays for all services performed through the termination date; Contractor removes its equipment and returns any Client property/credentials within {5} business days.

## 11. Liability, Indemnification & Pre-Existing Conditions

- Each party indemnifies the other against third-party claims for bodily injury or property damage to the extent caused by its own negligence or willful misconduct.
- Contractor promptly reports any damage its staff causes and will repair, replace, or credit at its option; Client must report claims within {5} business days of discovery.
- **Pre-existing conditions** documented at the walkthrough — oxidation, failing paint/caulk, spalling concrete, loose mortar, wood rot, damaged seals, aged roofing/screens, existing stains — are **not** Contractor's responsibility. Cleaning may expose or highlight these; the risk disclosures in the Residential Agreement §8 and the Liability Waiver (File 13) apply equally to commercial surfaces.
- Except for indemnification, insurance proceeds, and confidentiality breaches, neither party's aggregate liability exceeds the **fees paid or payable in the {6} months preceding the claim**, and neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages (including lost profits or business interruption).
