## Your Prep List (5 minutes, big difference)

*Please do these before we arrive. They keep your property safe and let us spend your money on cleaning, not on moving your stuff.*

- [ ] **Close all windows and doors** — tightly. Soft washing sprays cleaning solution on the siding; we don't want any finding its way through a cracked window or a worn seal.
- [ ] **Move vehicles** out of the driveway and away from the areas we're cleaning (overspray and cleaning solution can spot a finish if left to dry).
- [ ] **Clear the work area** — patio furniture, grills, potted plants, toys, door mats, and anything loose. If it's too heavy to move, tell us and we'll work around it.
- [ ] **Pets inside.** High-pressure spray, chemicals, and wet slick surfaces are not a place for pets. Please keep them indoors the whole visit.
- [ ] **Turn the outdoor water spigot ON** and make sure we can reach it. (If you don't have a working spigot, let us know now — we may need to bring a tank.)
- [ ] **Unlock gates** or leave us the code, and clear a path to the side yard / backyard if those areas are in the job.
- [ ] **Point out delicate plants** or anything mounted on the walls or eaves — cameras, lights, satellite dish, flags, hanging baskets.
- [ ] **Retract awnings** and bring in cushions if you have them.

If you won't be home, a quick text the morning of confirming the spigot is on and gates are open is all we need.

## What to Expect on Service Day

- **Arrival window:** we'll come {DATE} between {ARRIVAL WINDOW}. Exterior work runs on a route, so exact time varies a little.
- **How long it takes:** about {ESTIMATED DURATION} for your job.
- **The right method for each surface:** we use **high pressure** on tough surfaces like concrete, and **low-pressure soft washing** on siding, roofs, and anything pressure could damage. Soft washing looks gentle because it is — it does the work with cleaning solution and dwell time, not force. That's the correct, safest way to clean those surfaces.
- **It'll look wet (and a little sudsy) first.** Surfaces darken while wet and lighten as they dry — the real result shows once everything is dry.
- **When we're done:** we do a final walkthrough, rinse down plants and hardscape near the work, pack up, and send you an "all done" message with a couple of after photos.

**Please stay off freshly washed hard surfaces until they dry** — wet concrete and decking are slippery.

## An Honest Word on Results

We'd rather set expectations now than disappoint you later:

- **Some stains don't fully lift.** Rust, oil/grease, paint, irrigation (sprinkler mineral) stains, tannin bleed, and deep-set organic staining can be stubborn — we'll treat them and tell you honestly what to expect. If we didn't think we could improve it, we said so in your quote.
- **Clean can reveal age.** On older or faded ("chalky") siding, washing can highlight oxidation and wear that dirt was hiding. On older wood, cleaning can raise the grain slightly. None of this is damage — it's the surface's real condition showing through once it's clean.
- **We document before we start.** If we notice pre-existing issues — peeling paint, loose boards or mortar, cracked concrete, worn window seals, damaged screens — we photograph them before we begin. It protects you and us.

## Weather

Pressure washing is weather-dependent. We won't wash in conditions that compromise the work or safety — steady rain (it prevents cleaning solution from doing its job), high wind (it scatters overspray), freezing temperatures, or lightning. If we need to move your date, we'll reach out and reschedule to the next good day at no penalty. A rinse of clean rain after the job won't hurt anything.
