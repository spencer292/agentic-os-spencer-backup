# Link-Building Playbook — 8 Weeks (US-Focused)

## The Narrative for Spencer

> "Link building in 2026 is not about buying 100 junk links. It's about earning the right 30-40 links from places that actually matter for a US home-service business — directory citations (for local search), industry authority sites (for trust), government / education pages (for E-E-A-T), and content partnerships (for real traffic).
>
> Google's local ranking algorithm weighs NAP consistency — your Name, Address, Phone showing up identically across trusted directories — more than almost anything else. And for AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), being listed on government and industry authority pages is what gets you cited.
>
> This plan gets Got Moles listed on ~35 high-authority US sites in 8 weeks, with a clear rhythm for ongoing outreach. Some are one-time set-and-forget submissions. Others are quarterly or weekly habits. You own the list; I execute the work and flag decisions."

---

## The Rules of US Link Building (Read First)

1. **NAP must be identical everywhere.** Name: `Got Moles` (exactly, with the question mark if that's your legal name — confirm). Address: one canonical format. Phone: one number, no alternates. Variations kill local rankings.
2. **Never buy links.** Google penalties are permanent. A PBN or fiverr gig will destroy 15 years of ranking.
3. **Earn, don't exchange.** The phrase "link swap" is dead. Editorial links from partners (real recommendations) are fine. Reciprocal "I link to you, you link to me" scheme-style exchanges are not.
4. **Local authority beats global authority.** A link from the Sammamish Chamber of Commerce is worth more to you than a link from Forbes.
5. **Industry authority beats local authority.** A link from the National Wildlife Control Operators Association is worth more than 50 local directories combined.
6. **Government and education (.gov / .edu) links are rocket fuel.** Washington State University Extension and WA Department of Fish and Wildlife are achievable targets.
7. **Content is the bait.** The reason someone links to you is because you wrote something useful. No shortcuts.
8. **Don't obsess over Domain Authority scores.** DA is a third-party metric, not a Google metric. Focus on relevance and trust.

- Journalists post queries ("I need a quote from a pest control expert about mole damage")
- You pitch a 100-200 word expert response
- If selected, they publish the quote + link to got-moles.com
- Result: one link from a real publication (HuffPost, MSN, Today's Homeowner, Family Handyman, etc.)

# Part F — What to NOT Do

- ❌ Don't submit to every directory you see — 80% of sites that spam you are low-value PBNs
- ❌ Don't buy "500 backlinks for $99" — instant Google penalty
- ❌ Don't exchange links with unrelated sites (a dentist in Texas, a plumber in Florida)
- ❌ Don't use the same description verbatim on every site — rotate 3-4 variations
- ❌ Don't forget to track submissions — use a simple spreadsheet with URL, date submitted, status, live URL of listing
- ❌ Don't ignore listings once submitted — half the value is keeping them accurate and responding to reviews
- ❌ Don't over-optimize anchor text — most links should use "Got Moles" as anchor, not "Seattle mole control specialist" (Google flags this as manipulation)
