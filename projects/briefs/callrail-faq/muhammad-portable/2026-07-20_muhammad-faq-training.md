# Got Moles Phone Training — FAQ & Scenario Guide for Muhammad

**Built from real calls:** 203 transcribed phone calls over 90 seconds, April 30 – July 20, 2026 (every substantive call CallRail has recorded). Frequency counts below are actual counts from those calls, and every example quote is something a real caller said.

**The big picture:** roughly 60–70% of calls currently hit the AI answering service, which can only take a message — it can't quote prices, schedule, or answer questions. Nearly every question in this guide was asked on a live call, and live calls are where bookings happen. Your job is to be the human who answers on the first ring and can handle everything below.

## 1. The price sheet — know this cold

*(As quoted on calls through July 2026 — confirm current numbers with Spencer before your first shift.)*

**Option 1 — Quick Fix (one month):** ~5 weekly visits, $150 setup fee charged up front as the deposit. **Residential: $450 up to 1 acre, $500 for 1–3 acres, $600 for 3–5 acres.** **If no moles are caught, the $150 is all they pay** — same guarantee at every size. Flat rate no matter how many moles — "from one to a million moles, the price doesn't go up or down."

**Option 2 — Total Mole Control (annual):** Residential: $100/month up to 1 acre, $125 to 3 acres, $150 to 5 acres. Unlimited visits, no setup fee/deposit, billed the last day of each month. 12-month commitment, auto-renews month-to-month after. Early cancel = 50% of the remaining contract. Spencer's line: *"For $3 a day, I'll trap the mole for the whole year."*

**NO phone pricing for commercial properties (any size) or residential over 5 acres — Spencer bids those in person.** Always establish property type (residential/commercial) and approximate size before quoting anything.

**Bridge between them:** a hesitant caller can start with the Quick Fix, and the $150 setup fee credits toward the annual program if they convert.

**Discounts actually given on these calls:** senior/military/veteran ~10–15% (e.g., $85–90/mo), returning-customer month at $375, referral = free month per signed-up neighbor, group rates for neighbors ($85/2 yards, $75/3, $50/4–5 per yard), ~$1,000 for full-year prepay. Spencer volunteers these to build goodwill — ask him how much latitude you have.

## 3. The live-call script (Spencer runs this nearly verbatim — learn the arc)

1. **Open:** "What do you got going on out there?"
2. **Qualify:** location → residential or commercial? → property size ("over or under an acre?" — get rough acreage; it sets the price tier) → **"How long have you been dealing with the moles?"** ← this question drives the recommendation
3. **Educate** (this is the conversion engine): moles are territorial and solitary; tunnels work like a net to catch worms; a mole eats ~70% of its body weight daily; watering in summer = dinner bell; no one-and-done fix — "not if, but when" they return. Spencer's own story: his four acres in Enumclaw is where the business started.
4. **Pitch both options:** first-time/occasional problem → Quick Fix as a "benchmark." Fighting them a year or more → skip straight to annual: *"If you've been fighting moles for a year, I'm not even going to tell you about the quick fix."*
5. **Capture:** name, cell, email, address + **"How did you find me?"** (always ask — it's attribution data and a rapport moment)
6. **Close:** text/email the quote → approve → schedule, usually same/next day. Don't chase price-shoppers; hold price, explain overhead (crew, insurance, bonds), leave the door open: they often call back.

**Three reassurances that close deals over and over:** (1) nobody needs to be home, (2) no chemicals or poison — safe for pets and kids, (3) flat rate, no per-mole games, only $150 if nothing is caught.

**DIY validation:** most callers already failed with castor-oil pellets, poison gummy worms, sonic spikes, smoke bombs, or sprays. Don't mock — validate: *"That stuff doesn't work... this industry is full of gimmicks."* Why poison fails: moles smell chemicals — *"imagine you eat wagyu beef every day and I try to trick you with a Beyond Meat steak."* Nothing gets rid of earthworms, so nothing gets rid of the food supply.

## 4. Scenario playbook (from real calls)

**New-customer inquiry** — run the script in §3. Most common call type by far.

**Existing customer: scheduling/ETA/"tech question"** — the AI currently strands these people ("I just need somebody to put me on the list for tomorrow" → hung up annoyed). Answer live, check the schedule, give the notification-flow answer for ETAs. Photos of new activity go by text to the business cell.

**Retreatment ("they're back")** — normal, expected, warm lead. Returning-customer rate $375, or bridge to annual. Book fast.

**No-show complaint** — a missed appointment with no text generated two angry calls from the same customer in one morning. Apologize, reschedule immediately, and flag internally. A proactive text prevents the whole scenario.

**Upset caller** — rare (2–3 in three months, mostly at the AI, not the company). Let them vent, fix the concrete thing, don't argue.

**Out-of-area** — refer generously; it builds goodwill: Kitsap → Terry Cotton ("friendly competition, he's a good dude"); north/Snohomish → Kirk at Northwest Mole King; Vashon → honest "nobody serves the island" + DIY advice (Trapline traps / traplineproducts.com).

**Voles/gophers** — not our pest. Teach the ID rule, offer photo ID by text, refer to Trapline for DIY. Honest disqualification repeatedly earned "I appreciate your honesty."

**HOA / property manager / commercial** — different vocabulary: unit counts, vendor-compliance waivers, bids, board time windows. Capture requirements and hand to Spencer.

**Spousal sign-off** — a real close-blocker ("Let me forward this to my husband"). Send the quote to both, follow up next day.

**Callbacks on AI-captured leads** — the highest-value list you own, and it goes stale fast (one lead called twice on consecutive days because nobody called back). Rules: call back same day; **re-verify the address** (the AI confirmed "3302 Bond Road" twice to a caller saying "302" — caller left swearing); lead with pricing/scheduling since that's what they were blocked on.

**Junk calls** — vendors (CRM pitches, BBB accreditation), job applicants, pranks, dead air, garbled audio. Log and move on; garbled-audio calls deserve a careful callback since some are real customers.

## 5. Hard boundaries & house facts

- **Pests:** moles only. No voles, gophers, or general pest work (Rambo, Cascade Pest, Western Exterminator send moles TO us — they're partners, not competitors).
- **Area:** King/Pierce/Thurston. No Vashon, no Kitsap past ~Bremerton, no Snohomish. Kenmore/Bothell/Maltby = Mondays only. Routes are geographic (e.g., Burien Tuesdays, Sammamish Wednesdays).
- **Hours:** M–F business hours; no weekends; phones off 5pm–9am.
- **Methods messaging:** chemical-free, no poisons, safe for pets and children, professional trapping. On the legality question use Spencer's framing only (§2, #17). Never claim "WA's #1."
- **Identity:** all three Google listings (Tacoma, Enumclaw, Tukwila) are the same company. Veteran-owned. "219+ five-star Google reviews" is the safe claim.
- **Hazard notes:** anything a customer says about septic lines, irrigation, livestock, locked gates goes in the CRM as an impossible-to-miss note *before* scheduling.

## 6. Watch-outs with the systems

- **AI intake transcripts lie:** it garbles the company name (DotMoles, Gumballs, Gotmold...), mishears names and addresses, and logs wrong cities. **Re-verify every detail on callback.**
- **CallRail's "customer city" field is often flat wrong** (shows Atlanta/Boston for local callers) — trust only what the caller says.
- The ad phone number is a Google tracking number that **cannot receive texts** — photo ID requests go to the real business cell.
