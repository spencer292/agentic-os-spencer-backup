### Pillar 2: GEO / AI Citation Dominance

**The opportunity:** Zero competitors are optimizing for AI search. Got Moles is already ahead with llms.txt, schema triple-stack, and AI crawler permissions. Now push further.

**What AI engines cite:**
1. **Definition blocks in first 30% of content** — clear, factual, citable statements
2. **Named entities with credentials** — "Spencer Hill, US Army veteran, founder of Got Moles (est. 2017)"
3. **Specific statistics** — "5,000+ clients, 219+ reviews, 9 years, ~500 active subscribers"
4. **FAQ sections with FAQPage schema** — question-format content is prime AI extraction territory
5. **Authoritative backlinks** — .gov, .edu, industry association links signal trustworthiness

**Actions:**
- Every new blog post must include: definition block, FAQ section (3-5 questions), named entities, specific numbers
- Create Wikidata entity for Got Moles immediately — brands with Wikidata items are 3.2x more likely to get Knowledge Panel, 2.7x more likely in AI citations
- Publish original research: "State of Mole Control in Western Washington" annual report using Got Moles' data from 5,000+ jobs
- Target university extension citation: WSU Extension's mole management guide (pubs.extension.wsu.edu) — get cited as a professional resource
- Myth-busting content is the #1 performing format for AI citations — prioritize Tier 1 items 3 and 4

**Ask Maps preparation (Google's AI-powered local answers):**
- Ask Maps reads reviews, website content, and GBP data to answer natural-language queries
- Keyword-rich reviews and FAQ schema on the website are direct GEO inputs
- Coach customers to mention specific services in reviews (see Pillar 4)

1. Ask Maps (Gemini-powered) reads reviews + website + GBP data to answer queries

3. Review recency matters more than star rating — 31% of consumers require 4.5+ stars

**GBP optimization:**
- Verify primary category "Pest Control Service" on all 3 locations
- Add all relevant secondary categories

- Services tab with all 5 service lines + pricing

**Strategy: Barbell approach**

**Impact:** YouTube descriptions and transcripts feed directly into AI answers. A channel with 50+ videos becomes a massive GEO asset. YouTube videos also appear in Google search results, doubling organic real estate.

## National Authority Play (Content-First)

Got Moles doesn't need to service every state to own the national conversation.

**State-by-state mole control guides (35-40 states):**
- "Mole Control in [State]: Laws, Species, Best Methods"
- Each state has different mole species, different trapping laws, different soil conditions
- This is the exact playbook divorce mediation companies use to dominate national search with local content
- Each guide links back to Got Moles as the authority source

**Annual "State of Mole Control" report:**
- Use Got Moles' data from 5,000+ jobs: busiest months, most common species, average tunnel count, recurrence rates
- Publish as downloadable PDF + blog post + press release
- This becomes the cited source for journalists, AI engines, and industry publications

**Wikidata + Knowledge Panel:**
- Create Wikidata entity for Got Moles immediately
- Connect to GBP, website, social profiles
- This signals entity authority to all AI systems
