# Is Mole Poison Safe for Dogs? What Pet Owners Need to Know

**Author:** Spencer Hill, Got Moles
**Last Updated:** {publish date}

---

## [BLUF Opening]

Mole poison is not safe for dogs. Most mole poison baits contain bromethalin or zinc phosphide — both of which are toxic to dogs if ingested directly or through secondary poisoning (eating a poisoned mole). Symptoms of mole bait poisoning in dogs include vomiting, tremors, seizures, and in severe cases, death. Got Moles uses only chemical-free trapping methods that pose zero risk to pets.

## What's in Mole Poison?

The most common mole poisons sold in Washington include:

**Bromethalin** (found in Talpirid and similar products) — a neurotoxin that causes brain swelling. There is no antidote. Dogs that ingest bromethalin-based baits can develop tremors, seizures, paralysis, and fatal brain damage. Even small doses are dangerous.

**Zinc phosphide** — reacts with stomach acid to produce phosphine gas, which is toxic to all mammals. Ingestion causes vomiting, abdominal pain, seizures, and organ failure. Dogs can be poisoned by eating the bait directly or by eating a mole that has consumed the bait (secondary poisoning).

**Anticoagulants** — less common in mole baits but present in some rodent products used in tunnels. These prevent blood clotting and cause internal bleeding. A dog exposed to anticoagulants can bleed internally with no visible symptoms until it's too late.

---

## How Dogs Get Exposed

Dogs don't have to eat the bait directly. There are three ways they get exposed:

1. **Direct ingestion** — dogs dig up and eat the bait itself. Mole baits are designed to look and smell like earthworms. Dogs find that appealing.
2. **Secondary poisoning** — dogs eat a dead or dying mole that consumed poison. The toxin transfers through the mole's body.
3. **Soil contact** — some products dissolve into the soil. Dogs that dig in treated areas and lick their paws can ingest trace amounts.

If your dog spends time in the yard — and most do — mole poison creates a risk that doesn't go away when the application is done.

## What to Do If Your Dog Eats Mole Poison

If you suspect your dog has ingested mole bait or a poisoned mole:

1. **Call your vet or the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center immediately** — (888) 426-4435
2. **Do not wait for symptoms** — some poisons (especially bromethalin) cause irreversible damage before symptoms appear
3. **Bring the product packaging** if possible — the active ingredient determines the treatment
4. **Do not induce vomiting** unless directed by a veterinarian

## FAQ

**Can my dog get sick from eating a mole?**
A healthy, unpoisoned mole is unlikely to make your dog seriously ill, though moles can carry parasites. But if the mole was poisoned — by your bait or a neighbour's — your dog is at risk of secondary poisoning. That can be fatal.

**Is Talpirid safe for dogs?**
No. Talpirid contains bromethalin, a neurotoxin with no antidote. If a dog ingests a Talpirid bait worm, contact your veterinarian immediately.
