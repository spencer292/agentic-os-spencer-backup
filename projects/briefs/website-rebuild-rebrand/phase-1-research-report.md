### Where Got Moles Wins
1. **TMCP is unique** — no competitor has a comparable monthly subscription program
2. **The guarantee** ($150 setup only if no moles caught) is a strong risk-reversal
3. **Veteran-owned** is a genuine trust signal that resonates in the PNW

1. **Mole Patrol** is older (1983 vs 2017) and also mole-exclusive — "the original" positioning threat
2. **Mole Masters** has 30+ years and claims "premier" status with a lower price point
3. **Review count is low** — 13 Yelp / ~18-20 Google vs Moody Moles' 17 Yelp and Mole Patrol's 25 Yelp
4. **Kirkland market** has three competitors: Moody Moles, The Mole Guyz, Willard's — crowded
5. **Price perception** — $450 flat rate vs per-mole pricing makes Got Moles look expensive for single-mole jobs

### Opportunities
1. The TMCP page doesn't exist yet — nobody is targeting "mole control subscription" keywords
2. Most competitors have weak or nonexistent GEO — first mover advantage is real
3. Only Got Moles has a named, story-driven founder with military credentials (E-E-A-T gold)

6. **Named entity signals** — company name, founder name, location, credentials clearly stated
7. **Third-party citation building** — reviews on Google, Yelp, Nextdoor, BBB, Angi all feed AI citation confidence

## 6. WASHINGTON STATE LEGAL CONTEXT

### Initiative 713 (Critical for Content Strategy)

- **Passed 2000** — bans body-gripping, body-piercing, and steel-jawed leghold traps for all mammals
- **AG opinion (2002):** Any trap that holds, pierces, grasps, or pinches a body part is banned (except "common rat and mouse traps")
- **Legal methods:** Cage traps and common rat/mouse traps only
- **Still in effect 2026** — no substantial modification since passage
- **Implication for Got Moles:** Must be very careful about describing methods. Cannot mention body-gripping traps. Should position as compliant and knowledgeable about WA law.

**Content opportunity:** A dedicated article on "Are Mole Traps Legal in Washington State?" would rank well and build authority. Most competitors don't address this directly.
