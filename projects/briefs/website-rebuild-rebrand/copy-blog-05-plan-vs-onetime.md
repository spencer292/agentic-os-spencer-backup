## Why Moles Come Back

A cleared yard doesn't stay cleared by default. Here's why:

**Moles don't respect property lines.** Your property sits within a larger territory. When a mole is removed, the empty territory becomes available for neighboring moles to move into. That's biology, not bad luck.

**Your yard is attractive.** If the conditions that drew the first mole are still there — moist soil, healthy earthworm populations, well-maintained lawn — the next mole will find them just as appealing.

**Seasonal pressure.** Mole activity peaks in spring and autumn in Western Washington. Even a yard that's been clear for months can see new activity when the season turns.

We see this pattern constantly. A homeowner calls, we clear the moles, the yard looks great. Three to six months later, the phone rings again. Same yard. New moles. Same frustration.

**Choose one-time removal if:**
