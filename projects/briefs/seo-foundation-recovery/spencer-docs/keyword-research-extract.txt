========== Methodology ==========

METHODOLOGY & NOTES | 
Read this first. Volumes are calibrated estimates, not Google Keyword Planner exports. | 
Section | Detail
VOLUME ESTIMATE METHOD | Volume ranges are calibrated based on: (a) SERP density observed during this research session, (b) Western WA population (~4.5M), (c) PNW mole prevalence (Townsend's stronghold = elevated demand vs national baselines), (d) seasonality of Mar-May & Sep-Nov peaks, (e) competitor page volume, (f) public Google Trends signals. These are NOT precision Google Keyword Planner numbers. Validate with GKP and your actual GSC data.
PRIORITY SCORING | P1 = high commercial intent + winnable + meaningful volume. Attack first. P2 = solid commercial signal + attainable. P3 = long tail, brand defense, or low effort. Score = (Volume × Commercial Intent × Winnability) — but with a HEAVY thumb on the scale for keywords your existing strong content already serves (sunk-cost wins).
INTENT BUCKETS | Commercial = ready to buy (highest revenue value). Comparison = price/provider research (high commercial). Problem = mid-funnel DIY-then-pro pipeline. Informational = top-of-funnel awareness. Brand = defending your name. Each bucket needs a different content treatment.
SEARCH INTENT TRAP | 'Mole removal' alone returns ~90% dermatology results (skin moles). The yard/lawn/exterminator/control modifiers are mandatory disambiguators. Every page must signal pest intent in title, H1, first 200 words.
DIFFICULTY DEFINITIONS | Very Low = no real competitor; Low = competitors but weak content; Medium = competing strong sites of similar age; High = entrenched competitors with domain authority; Very High = national chains (Croach) or unrelated industries (dermatology) dominating.
STATUS DEFINITIONS | STRONG = your page is the best in market. HAVE IT = you have a page, may need optimization. PARTIAL = you cover the topic but split across pages or under-optimized. WEAK = you target loosely but not as primary keyword. GAP/MISSING = no dedicated page exists.
SEASONALITY | Western WA mole search behavior peaks twice yearly: Mar-May (spring dispersal, fresh mounds appearing) and Sep-Nov (autumn rains return, mounds reappear). Troughs in Jun-Aug (moles dig deeper, less surface activity) and Dec-Feb (frozen ground masks activity). Plan content publishing for Feb and Aug — 4-6 weeks before each peak.
KEY COMPETITORS BY KEYWORD | Mole Masters: dominates 'yard mole control [city]' (49 thin pages). Mole Patrol: dominates 'mole exterminator seattle' (42-yr domain). Moody Moles: weak Wix, easy to beat. NW Mole Pros: WordPress, identical templated pages. NW Mole King: broken site (Yext placeholder errors). The Mole Man (Murray, Puyallup): 25-yr local brand. Mole Control & More: South King since 2000. Mole Busters: founded 2024, weakest threat. Croach: national chain w/ Seattle pages.
WHAT TO DO FIRST | 1) Fix URL cannibalization (per fix doc). 2) Add 'yard' + 'exterminator' to homepage title/H1. 3) Verify GBP rankings for 'mole control near me' across Seattle/Tacoma/Bellevue. 4) Run Google Search Console — pull 'queries' report for last 90 days, identify what's already getting impressions but not clicks. 5) THEN attack P1 keywords from this sheet.
WHAT THIS DOC IS NOT | Not a Google Ads keyword plan (PPC bidding requires GKP-pulled CPCs). Not a comprehensive long-tail map (you'd need Ahrefs/Semrush for that — 1,000s of variants). Not a content calendar (separate exercise). Not a measurement plan (set up GA4 + GSC + rank-tracking in Mangools or similar).
VALIDATION TASKS | Required before acting: (1) Pull last 90 days from Google Search Console — sort by clicks descending, then by impressions descending. (2) Run Google Keyword Planner on top 20 keywords here — get exact GKP ranges. (3) Check your current rank for each P1 keyword via incognito Google search from a Seattle IP (or use Mangools/Ahrefs). (4) Audit your GBP profile rankings in Local Pack.
