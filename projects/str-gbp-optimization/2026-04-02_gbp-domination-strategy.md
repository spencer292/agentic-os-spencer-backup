## Executive Summary

Got Moles is in a strong position with 219+ five-star reviews across 3 locations and zero competitors doing GEO. The opportunity is to go from "good GBP presence" to "untouchable local dominance" by exploiting three shifts happening in 2026:

1. **Google replaced Q&A with "Ask Maps" (Gemini-powered)** — AI now reads your reviews, website, and GBP data to answer natural-language questions. Businesses that feed the AI the best source material win.
2. **Dynamic profiles are the new ranking factor** — Google rewards businesses that "look alive" (posts, photos, review velocity, engagement signals). Static listings are becoming invisible.
3. **Review expectations jumped dramatically** — 31% of consumers now require 4.5+ stars (up from 17% last year). 89% expect a response to their review. Review recency matters more than star rating.

Got Moles' competitive moat: specialist focus, TMCP subscription model, veteran story, and review volume. No competitor matches all four. The strategy below turns that moat into local pack dominance.

## 1. GBP Optimization — What Drives Visibility in 2026

### The Ranking Formula

Google's local algorithm still runs on three pillars, but the weighting has shifted:

| Factor | Weight | What it means for Got Moles |
|--------|--------|-----------------------------|
| **GBP Signals** | 32% | Primary category, business hours, completeness, attributes |
| **Reviews** | 20% (up from 16% in 2023) | Volume, velocity, keywords in reviews, sentiment, response rate |
| **Behavioral/Engagement** | Rising fast | Clicks, calls, direction requests, post engagement, profile dwell time |
| **On-site SEO** | ~15% | Landing page quality, NAP consistency, service page alignment |
| **Proximity** | Variable | Distance from searcher — can't control, but can mitigate with multi-location strategy |
| **Links/Citations** | ~10% | Third-party mentions, directory listings, local press |

### Immediate Optimization Checklist

**Primary category:** "Pest Control Service" — verify this is set correctly on all 3 locations. This is the single most influential ranking factor.

**Additional categories:** Add all relevant secondary categories. Businesses with precise category alignment get 66% more profile interactions.

**Business description:** Each location needs a unique description. Mention the city/neighborhood. Include "mole control," "mole removal," "mole trapping" naturally. This feeds Ask Maps.

**Services tab — CRITICAL for 2026:** Google cross-references your website's Services page with your GBP Services tab to verify expertise. Each location should list:
- Mole Inspection & Assessment
- One-Month Mole Eradication Program
- Total Mole Control Program (TMCP) — Monthly Service
- Emergency Mole Removal
- Commercial/HOA Mole Control
- Include pricing where possible ($150 setup, $100/month TMCP)

**Products tab:** Add the TMCP as a "product" with description, pricing, and a photo. This gives Google additional structured data to surface in Ask Maps answers.

**Attributes to set:**
- Veteran-owned (this is an E-E-A-T signal and a trust differentiator)
- Locally owned
- Onsite services
- Online estimates/appointments (if applicable)
- Service area definition for each location

**Business hours:** Being open when users search is now the No. 5 local pack ranking factor. Make sure hours are accurate, including holiday hours. Consider extended hours listings if Spencer's team takes calls early/late.

### GBP Posts Strategy

Posts still matter — not for direct ranking weight, but because they signal an "alive" business and drive engagement signals that DO rank. Businesses posting weekly see 20% higher map-pack consistency.

**Posting cadence:** 2-3 posts per week across all 3 locations.

**What performs best:**
- **Event/offer posts** get 2x engagement vs. standard updates
- **Before/after photos** of yards (damage vs. clean lawn post-service)
- **Seasonal tips** ("Spring is peak mole season in Western WA — here's why")
- **Customer success stories** (with permission — "Jennifer in Sammamish had 14 mounds...")
- **Spencer's expertise** (short "Did you know?" mole behavior facts)
- **TMCP enrollment prompts** with clear CTA

**Post template rotation:**
1. Monday: Customer story/testimonial highlight
2. Wednesday: Mole fact or seasonal tip (positions Spencer as specialist)
3. Friday: Offer or TMCP promotion

**Each location gets unique posts** — do NOT copy the same post across all 3. Google notices and it looks spammy. Localize each post to the area that location serves.

### Photos & Videos

Listings with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks.

**Photo targets per location:**
- Minimum 3 service photos (technician setting traps, inspecting yard, results)
- Minimum 3 "product" photos (traps, equipment, branded vehicle)
- Team photos (Spencer, technicians — builds trust)
- Before/after yard photos (the money shot for mole control)
- Office/vehicle exterior (establishes legitimacy)

**Upload cadence:** Add 1-2 new photos per week per location. Fresh imagery signals activity to Google.

**Video:** Low adoption rate among businesses — this is a gap Got Moles can exploit. Short 30-60 second videos of:
- Spencer explaining the TMCP
- Technician on-site walkthrough
- Customer testimonial clips
- Mole behavior explainers

**Geotagging:** Geotag all photos to associate them with your exact service area. Tools like GeoImgr or manual EXIF editing work.

### Q&A Is Dead — Ask Maps Is Here

Google discontinued Q&A in December 2025 and replaced it with "Ask Maps" powered by Gemini. This is a fundamental shift.

**What Ask Maps does:** Users ask natural-language questions like "Who's the best mole removal company near Sammamish?" and Gemini generates a narrative answer by reading your GBP data, reviews, website, and third-party sources.

**How to win with Ask Maps:**

1. **Feed it structured FAQ data on your website.** Since Q&A is gone, your website must host FAQ content in schema-marked-up format. Every question a customer might ask Google about mole control should have a clear answer on got-moles.com.

2. **Schema markup is non-negotiable.** FAQ schema, LocalBusiness schema, Service schema, Review schema — Gemini reads structured data more accurately than raw text.

3. **Review content becomes the AI's source of truth.** When someone asks "Is Got Moles safe for pets?", Gemini will pull from reviews that mention pet safety. This makes keyword-rich reviews a GEO strategy (see Section 3).

4. **Test Ask Maps yourself.** Search for mole control queries in Google Maps and see what Gemini says about Got Moles. If the AI is pulling inaccurate info, the fix is almost always: update your GBP data, get reviews that mention the correct info, or add the answer to your website.

## 2. Multi-Location Strategy — 3 Locations Without Cannibalization

### The Challenge

Service area businesses face a proximity problem: Google's local algorithm ranks businesses based on distance from the searcher. With 3 locations, Got Moles needs each location to "own" its geographic territory without competing against itself.

### Location Territory Strategy

Each location should have a clearly defined service area with minimal overlap:

**Location 1 (Enumclaw area):** Focus on South King County, Pierce County foothills, Auburn, Kent, Maple Valley, Black Diamond, Buckley

**Location 2 (if North/Central):** Focus on Eastside, Seattle suburbs, Bellevue, Kirkland, Redmond, Sammamish, Issaquah, Woodinville

**Location 3 (if South/Tacoma area):** Focus on Tacoma, Puyallup, Lakewood, Olympia corridor, Federal Way, Bonney Lake

**Configuration rules:**
- Set service areas using city names (up to 20 per location), not radius
- Avoid overlapping the same city across multiple locations
- Smaller service areas in dense urban markets, larger in suburban/rural

### Unique Content Per Location

Copy-paste listings with only the city name swapped do NOT work. Each location needs:

- **Unique business description** mentioning the specific neighborhoods/cities served
- **Unique posts** referencing local events, weather, or community
- **Unique photos** showing work in that area
- **Location-specific reviews** (customers from that area reviewing that location)
- **Dedicated landing page on the website** — each GBP links to a unique city/area page, NOT the homepage

### City Pages on the Website

For cities without a physical location, city pages are the path to organic (not local pack) visibility:

- Create 10-15 high-quality city pages targeting "[city] mole control" / "[city] mole removal"
- Each page needs 100% unique content — real local context, not templated fill-in-the-blank
- Include: specific mole species/behavior in that area, local soil conditions, local testimonials, service details specific to that area
- This strategy gets organic rankings, which feed prominence signals back to GBP

### What Won't Work

- Setting the same service area across all 3 locations (cannibalization)
- Having all 3 locations link to the same website page (dilutes relevance signals)
- Posting identical content across all 3 listings (looks spammy)

## 3. Review Strategy — From 219 to Dominance

### Current Position

219+ five-star reviews across 3 locations is strong, but the competitive landscape is tightening:
- **Mole Masters** has 45 Yelp reviews + Google reviews (30+ years in business)
- **Mole Control & More** has been operating since 2000
- **The Mole Man** has 25+ years of operation

Got Moles' advantage: specialist positioning + TMCP creates a recurring review opportunity that one-time-service competitors can't match.

### Optimal Review Velocity

- **Minimum:** 1 new review per week per location (businesses hitting this benchmark rank 25% higher)
- **Target:** 3-5 new reviews per month per location
- **Steady beats spiky.** A burst of 20 reviews in one week then silence for 2 months is worse than 2 reviews per week consistently
- **67% of consumers say recency is more influential than star rating** when choosing a local business

### Systematic Review Generation (Google-Compliant)

**The TMCP Advantage:** With ~500 active monthly subscribers, Got Moles has a built-in review engine that no competitor can match. Every monthly service visit is a review opportunity.

**Automated flow:**
1. **After every service visit**, send a follow-up text/email within 24-48 hours
2. Use Google's official review link and QR code (now natively available in GBP — click "Ask for reviews" in your profile)
3. Include a direct link in the message — one tap to leave a review
4. Track which location served the customer and send them to THAT location's review link

**In-person asks:**
- Train technicians to ask at the end of every visit: "We'd really appreciate a Google review — I'll text you a link"
- Print QR codes on service completion cards/door hangers
- Place QR code on the truck wrap (visible while parked at customer's house)

**Post-eradication follow-up:**
- 30 days after initial service completion, send a "How's your yard looking?" check-in with a review prompt
- This catches customers at peak satisfaction (mole-free yard for a month)

**TMCP renewal touchpoint:**
- At annual renewal or milestone (6 months, 12 months), prompt for a review
- Angle: "You've been mole-free for [X months] — mind sharing your experience?"

**What NOT to do:**
- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google guidelines)
- Never filter — don't send the link only to happy customers
- Never ask for a "five-star review" — ask for an honest review
- Never gate reviews behind a satisfaction survey (Google considers this filtering)

### Review Keyword Strategy

Google's NLP analyzes review content for keywords and surfaces them in search results and AI answers. Got Moles should coach (not script) customers to mention specific things:

**High-value keywords to naturally appear in reviews:**
- City/neighborhood name ("Our yard in Sammamish...")
- Service type ("mole removal," "mole trapping," "mole control")
- TMCP mention ("monthly program," "ongoing protection")
- Safety mentions ("safe for our dog," "pet-friendly," "no chemicals")
- Speed/reliability ("came out the next day," "always on time")
- Guarantee mention ("they guarantee their work")
- Specialist angle ("they only do moles," "mole experts")

**How to naturally generate keyword-rich reviews:**
- In the review request message, include a prompt: "What was the mole problem like before we came, and how's your yard now?" This naturally produces before/after language with service keywords.
- Train technicians to use specific language during service ("I'm setting up your mole control program today") so customers echo it in reviews.

### Review Response Strategy

89% of consumers expect a response. 19% expect same-day. Google monitors response time.

**Response rules:**
- Respond to EVERY review within 24 hours (positive and negative)
- Use the reviewer's name
- Reference specific details from their review (not canned responses)
- Include a keyword naturally: "Glad the mole control program is keeping your [City] yard clear!"
- Keep it conversational, not promotional
- For negative reviews: acknowledge, apologize, offer resolution offline, never argue publicly
- Sign with a name (Spencer or the responding team member)

**Response template framework (adapt per review, never copy-paste):**

Positive: "[Name], great to hear your [City] yard is mole-free! [Specific reference to their review]. We appreciate you trusting Got Moles — Spencer and the team."

Negative: "[Name], we're sorry to hear about this experience. That's not the standard we hold ourselves to. Please reach out to us directly at [phone] so we can make this right."

### Third-Party Review Platforms

36% of consumers check 2+ review sites. 41% check 3+. Got Moles needs presence beyond Google:

| Platform | Priority | Why | Current status |
|----------|----------|-----|----------------|
| **Google** | #1 | Primary local ranking factor + AI Overview source | 219+ reviews |
| **Yelp** | #2 | 13 reviews (WEAK — needs attention). Yelp reviews feed AI answers. | 13 reviews |
| **BBB** | #3 | Trust signal, especially for older homeowners. Accreditation adds legitimacy. | Check status |
| **Nextdoor** | #4 | Hyperlocal. Got Moles' ICP lives on Nextdoor. Business recommendations carry weight. | Check status |
| **Angi / HomeAdvisor** | #5 | Usage increased in 2026. Home services category match. | Check status |
| **Facebook** | #6 | Social proof + AI citation source. | Check status |
| **Apple Maps** | #7 | Growing platform. Separate from Google ecosystem. | Check listing |

**Yelp is the urgent gap.** 13 reviews vs. Mole Masters' 45. Yelp reviews feed into AI answers (Perplexity specifically cites Yelp for local businesses). The fix: add a Yelp link as an occasional alternative in review requests. Don't always send to Google — rotate 80/20 (Google/Yelp) until Yelp catches up.

## 5. GBP and AI Overviews / GEO

### How GBP Data Feeds AI

Google AI Overviews appear in 13%+ of all searches and growing. For local queries, the AI pulls from:

1. **GBP structured data** (categories, services, attributes, hours, description)
2. **Review content and sentiment** — the primary "source of truth" for AI answers
3. **Website content** — especially structured FAQ pages and service descriptions
4. **Third-party sources** — Yelp, BBB, Nextdoor, Reddit, news articles

When someone asks Google "What's the best mole removal company near [city]?", the AI constructs an answer from ALL of these sources.

### GEO Strategy for Got Moles (Zero Competitors Doing This)

This is the first-mover advantage. No mole control company in Western WA is optimizing for AI answers.

**Website-side GEO:**
- FAQ schema on every service page (answers the exact questions people ask AI)
- LocalBusiness schema on city pages
- Service schema with pricing
- Review schema (aggregate rating)
- "About Spencer" page with full E-E-A-T signals (veteran background, 15 years experience, 5,000 clients)

**Review-side GEO:**
- Keyword-rich reviews are indexed by AI and used as evidence
- A review saying "Got Moles is the go-to mole specialist in Western Washington" teaches the AI about their specialization
- A review saying "safe for my dog and kids, no chemicals" teaches the AI about safety
- The more reviews that mention the SAME themes, the more confident the AI becomes in surfacing Got Moles for those queries

**Content-side GEO:**
- Publish authoritative content about mole behavior, control methods, and Western WA-specific mole issues
- This content gets cited by AI when answering general mole questions, with Got Moles as the source
- Blog posts answering questions like "Are moles dangerous to pets?", "Why do I have moles in my yard?", "Do ultrasonic mole repellers work?" — these are AI answer bait

**Third-party GEO:**
- Perplexity specifically cites Reddit, Yelp, and forum posts
- Spencer or the team participating in r/Seattle, r/Tacoma, or Nextdoor discussions about mole problems = GEO signal
- Having a BBB profile with reviews = trust signal AI models weight

### Testing AI Visibility

Regularly test these queries in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews:
- "Best mole removal company in [city] Washington"
- "How to get rid of moles in my yard in Washington state"
- "Is mole trapping safe for pets?"
- "Mole control subscription service Washington"
- Document what appears and track changes monthly
