## LIFT Model Scores

### Value Proposition — 8/10 (weight: 30%)

**Strengths:**
- H1 includes the specific price ($100/month) and the key benefit (year-round protection). No ambiguity.
- Subheadline "The last mole conversation you'll ever need to have" is emotionally resonant for someone tired of the cycle.
- Price transparency in the hero is a trust signal. Jennifer doesn't have to hunt for the cost.
- "~500 homeowners already enrolled" provides social proof of adoption.

**Weaknesses:**
- The H1 is long (11 words + price). On mobile it will wrap significantly. Consider whether the price belongs in the H1 or in a separate price badge.
- "Total Mole Control Program" as a name is functional but not emotionally charged. Not necessarily a problem. Clarity beats cleverness.

**Recommendation (Test):**
- Test H1 with price in a separate element: H1 "YEAR-ROUND MOLE PROTECTION" + price badge "$100/mo" beside or below it. Keeps the H1 punchy while price remains above fold.

### Relevance — 9/10 (weight: 20%)

**Strengths:**
- Page serves visitors who clicked "Learn About the TMCP" from the homepage. Perfect match.
- Problem section (Section 2) directly addresses why one-time doesn't work. Meets Jennifer exactly where she is in her thinking.
- Comparison table (Section 6) helps her make the TMCP vs One-Time decision. She doesn't have to navigate away to compare.

**Weaknesses:**
- If Jennifer lands here from Google (searching "mole control subscription"), the page assumes she knows about Got Moles. The GEO definition block is missing from this blueprint. Add one.

**Recommendation (Fix now):**
- Add a GEO Definition Block as Section 1a (between hero and problem section): "The Total Mole Control Program (TMCP) is Got Moles' year-round mole protection service for homeowners in Western Washington. For $100 per month, enrolled homeowners receive regular monitoring visits, immediate response to new mole activity, and a detailed report after every check. Nearly 500 homeowners are currently enrolled."

### Clarity — 8/10 (weight: 20%)

**Strengths:**
- Section structure follows a logical decision flow: problem → solution → what's included → pricing → comparison → proof → objections → close
- Each section has one job. Well-defined.
- Comparison table (TMCP vs One-Time) is a strong clarity element. Side-by-side makes the decision easy.

**Weaknesses:**
- 9 sections is a lot. On mobile, this is a long scroll. The visitor who arrived from the homepage has already seen the trust bar, problem section, and service overview. Some content here repeats what they've already processed.
- "What's Included" (Section 4) and "How the TMCP Works" (Section 3) could blur together. They both answer "what do I get?" Merge or differentiate more sharply.

**Recommendation (Fix now):**
- Merge "How the TMCP Works" (process) and "What's Included" (features) into one section: "What You Get." Lead with the process (4 steps), then bullet the inclusions below. Saves a scroll. Reduces from 9 sections to 8.
- Consider whether the comparison table (Section 6) should move UP to just after pricing (Section 5). Jennifer is thinking about cost. Show her the comparison immediately after she sees the price, not after social proof.

### Urgency — 6/10 (weight: 10%)

**Strengths:**
- Problem section ("Moles Always Come Back") creates internal urgency through the cycle-of-failure framing.

**Weaknesses:**
- No seasonal framing. "Moles are most active in spring. Enrolling now means your yard is protected before peak season." This is authentic urgency, not manufactured.
- No cost-of-waiting framing. "Every month without protection risks new mole damage to the lawn you've invested in."
- The page is informational. It explains well but doesn't push toward a decision.

**Recommendation (Fix now):**
- Add seasonal context to the problem section: "Moles don't take breaks. Western Washington's mild climate means activity year-round, with peaks in spring and fall."
- Add a cost-of-waiting line near pricing: "One month of unprotected mole activity can undo months of lawn maintenance."

### Anxiety — 8/10 (weight: 10%)

**Strengths:**
- "~500 homeowners already enrolled" is strong adoption proof
- TMCP-specific testimonials (Section 7) address the right concerns (peace of mind, reporting, ongoing protection)
- FAQ section handles cancellation, pausing, contracts. These are the real anxieties for a subscription.
- Pricing is transparent. No hidden fees.

**Weaknesses:**
- No guarantee statement specific to TMCP. The one-time removal has a guarantee ($150 setup fee only if no moles caught). What's the TMCP equivalent? If moles appear, do they come back immediately? This needs to be explicit.
- No "what happens if I cancel" clarity in the main body. It's in the FAQ but should also be addressed before the FAQ (anxiety hits before the visitor scrolls to FAQs).

**Recommendation (Fix now):**
- Add a TMCP-specific guarantee or commitment: "If you see new mole activity between visits, call us. We respond within [timeframe] at no extra charge." Place this near or in the pricing section.
- Add a "no long-term lock-in" message near pricing if accurate: "12-month initial commitment, then month-to-month." This reduces subscription anxiety.

### Distraction — 9/10 (weight: 10%)

**Strengths:**
- One primary CTA (call). Repeated at natural decision points (after how it works, after pricing, final CTA).
- Comparison table links to One-Time page but as informational, not as an escape hatch.
- No competing navigation beyond the standard header.

**Weaknesses:**
- None significant. Clean page structure.
