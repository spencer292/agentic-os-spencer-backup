## 1. YouTube Strategy

### The Competitive Landscape

The mole control YouTube space is **wide open**. There is no dedicated mole control channel with significant following. The closest comparison:

- **Shawn Woods** (Mousetrap Monday) — 2M subscribers, 611M+ views. Started with primitive archery, pivoted to pest trapping content. Proof that niche pest content scales massively. His content is primarily mice/rats, not moles.
- **DoMyOwnPestControl.com** — 60K subscribers. General pest control how-to.
- **NPMA** (National Pest Management Association) — 12.4K subscribers. Industry body, not a practitioner.

**The gap:** Nobody owns "mole guy on YouTube." Spencer can be the Shawn Woods of moles. This is a first-mover opportunity with zero meaningful competition.

## 2. Wikipedia and Knowledge Base Presence

### Can Got Moles Get a Wikipedia Page?

**Short answer: Not directly, not yet.** Wikipedia's notability guidelines require "significant coverage in multiple reliable secondary sources that are independent of the subject." A local mole control company doesn't meet this bar today.

**However, there are two powerful plays:**

### Play 1: Get Cited on Existing Wikipedia Articles

The [Mole (animal) Wikipedia article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mole_(animal)) covers pest control methods. If Got Moles publishes authoritative research or data (e.g., a mole behavior study, a regional mole population report, or a comprehensive trapping methodology guide), that content could be cited as a source on this article.

**Action steps:**
1. Publish a comprehensive "Mole Control Methods: A Professional Guide" on got-moles.com with original data from 5,000+ jobs
2. Get that guide cited by university extension programs (Washington State University, Oregon State) or wildlife agencies
3. Once cited by reliable secondary sources, the content becomes eligible for Wikipedia citation
4. Contribute edits to the mole control sections of the Wikipedia article, citing Got Moles' published research

### Play 2: Create a Wikidata Entity

This is the higher-leverage move for SEO and AI visibility.

**Why Wikidata matters:** Wikidata feeds structured entity data directly to Google's Knowledge Graph, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Bing, and every major AI search tool. A 2024 study found brands with verified Wikidata items were **3.2x more likely to display a Knowledge Panel** and **2.7x more likely to appear in AI Overview citations.**

**Action steps:**
1. Create a Wikidata entity for Got Moles with proper structured data:
   - `instance of`: pest control company
   - `industry`: pest control
   - `founded by`: Spencer (with his Wikidata entity)
   - `headquarters location`: Western Washington
   - `official website`: got-moles.com
   - `inception`: 2017
2. Back every statement with reference URLs from authoritative sources
3. Add the Wikidata Q-identifier URL to the `sameAs` array in Got Moles' Organization JSON-LD schema on the website
4. Create a Wikidata entity for Spencer as a pest control professional and veteran entrepreneur

### Play 3: Google Knowledge Panel

More than 50% of Knowledge Panel citations come from Wikipedia, but you can get a Knowledge Panel without Wikipedia:
- Consistent business information across all platforms (NAP consistency)
- Strong Google Business Profile (Got Moles has 219+ reviews — strong foundation)
- Wikidata entity (see above)
- Press coverage and independent media mentions
- Claim the Knowledge Panel once it appears via Google's verification process

## 6. National Authority Expansion

Got Moles doesn't need to service every state to become the national authority on mole control. Content authority and service delivery can be decoupled.

### Content-First National Strategy

**State-by-state mole control guides:**
Create comprehensive guides for every US state where moles are a problem (approximately 35-40 states). Each guide covers:
- Mole species in that state
- Peak mole seasons
- State-specific regulations on trapping
- Common soil types and how they affect mole behavior
- Recommended approach for that region
- "If you're in Western Washington, Got Moles can help directly. Elsewhere, here's what to look for in a mole control professional."

This creates massive topical authority and captures national search traffic. The divorce mediation industry uses this exact playbook — publishing state-by-state guides to establish themselves as the national authority.

**Industry thought leadership content:**
1. Annual "State of Mole Control" report — original data from 5,000+ jobs
2. "Mole Control Methods Compared" — definitive resource comparing trapping, chemical, sonic, and other methods
3. "The Economics of Mole Damage" — data on lawn repair costs, property value impact
4. "Chemical-Free Pest Control: The Future" — position Got Moles at the front of the industry trend

### Franchise / Licensing Model (Future)

If Got Moles wants to expand service delivery nationally, pest control franchising is proven and scalable:

**Franchise structure:**
- One-time franchise fee for territory rights
- Ongoing royalty (% of revenue)
- Monthly marketing fee for national brand campaigns
- Comprehensive training on Got Moles' methods
- Access to the TMCP subscription model system
- Spencer's methodology and protocols

**Key advantages Got Moles has for franchising:**
- The TMCP is a genuinely unique recurring revenue model
- Chemical-free methods are a growing market trend
- The brand has proof of concept (5,000 clients, 219+ reviews, 9 years)
- Mole-exclusive focus means simple training and operations

**Alternative to franchising — licensing/consulting:**
- License the Got Moles methodology and TMCP model to independent operators
